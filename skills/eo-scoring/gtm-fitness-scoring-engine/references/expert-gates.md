# Expert Framework Gates: Diagnostic & Strategic Context

Expert gates add strategic context to motion recommendations. They don't change scores but may reorder priorities, add prerequisites, or trigger interventions.

---

## Gate 1: Pattern of Inaction Early Check

**Trigger:** Founder has been working on idea >6 months AND has zero launches/products shipped to market.

**Detection signals during assessment:**
- High capability answers (Q1-Q6 averaging 3+) but no evidence of execution
- Answers include phrases like "still researching," "want to get it right," "building first"
- Expert archetype from SC4 with zero launched products
- SC1 positioning statement is aspirational (not grounded in real customer feedback)

**Diagnostic:**
The founder has domain knowledge, resources, or capability but is stuck in perfectionism. This is the #1 failure mode for domain experts and experienced operators entering entrepreneurship. In corporate, optimization and certainty before action are rewarded. In entrepreneurship, they're fatal.

**Output (added to gtm-fitness.md under "WARNINGS"):**

```
⚠️ WARNING: PATTERN OF INACTION DETECTED

Your capability scores are strong, but your execution history shows zero GTM
motion activation in the past 90 days. This is the #1 failure mode for domain
experts entering entrepreneurship.

ROOT CAUSE: You're optimizing for CERTAINTY before action. In corporate, that's
rewarded. In entrepreneurship, it kills momentum.

PRESCRIPTION — The 72-Hour Rule:
Your first GTM motion should be activated within 72 HOURS of completing this
assessment. Not 72 days. Not "after I finish building."

RECOMMENDED FIRST MOTION: [Lowest-friction motion from top 3]

ACTION: Set a calendar reminder for 72 hours from now. Commit to launching
something that moves.
```

**Impact:**
- Does NOT reduce scores (scores remain accurate)
- Adds a prerequisite gate to motion recommendations
- Prioritizes SPEED over comprehensiveness in motion sequencing
- Flags founder for accountability check at 72-hour mark

**Follow-up:**
Ask founder to commit to specific 72-hour action before leaving assessment. Document commitment.

## Gate 2: Content Systems Check (Matt Gray Framework)

**Applied when:** Any content-dependent motion (Authority Education, Build-in-Public, Wave Riding, BOFU SEO, Value Trust) scores as PRIMARY.

**Evaluation Questions:**
1. Does founder batch-create content or create ad-hoc?
2. Is there a scheduling/repurposing system in place?
3. How many weeks can founder sustain cadence without burnout?

**Assessment Table:**

| System Status | Implication | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **System exists** (batch creation, scheduling, repurposing) | Can sustain 2-3 content motions simultaneously | Proceed. Founder has infrastructure. |
| **Ad-hoc creation** (writes when inspired) | High burnout risk; can only sustain 1 motion | Add "Build 30-in-30 content batch before launch" as pre-launch task. Budget 1 week. |
| **No system** (nothing set up) | Content will die after 2-3 weeks | BLOCK: Require 1-week content system design BEFORE launching motion. Recommend "30-in-30" sprint. |

**The 30-in-30 Framework (Matt Gray):**
If content system doesn't exist, founder must batch-create 30 content pieces in 30 days (1 per day) before launching PRIMARY content motion:
- Week 1: Create framework/angles (brainstorm 30 topics)
- Week 2: Draft all 30 pieces
- Week 3: Record/write final versions
- Week 4: Schedule across 30 days (3-4 per week)

**Output (added to gtm-fitness.md):**

If system missing:
```
PREREQUISITE: Content Systems Design

Before activating [Authority Education/Build-in-Public] motion, you need a
sustainable content system. Recommend: 30-in-30 Sprint.

Spend 1 week batch-creating 30 content pieces → schedule across 4 weeks
→ then launch PRIMARY motion with 4 weeks of content banked.

This prevents burnout and ensures you don't go quiet after 3 weeks.
```

**Impact:**
- Adds 1-week prerequisite to content motions if system missing
- Prevents launch failure from content burnout
- Improves success rate of content-dependent motions

## Gate 3: Solo Founder Bandwidth Calculator

**Input:** Weekly hours available (from SC4 A3 or inferred from Q10 sales capacity, Q1 content frequency).

**Bandwidth Assumption Rules (Non-Negotiable):**

| Hours Available | PRIMARY Motions | SECONDARY Motions | Sequencing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **20+ hours/week** | 1 PRIMARY | +1 SECONDARY | Run simultaneously |
| **10-19 hours/week** | 1 PRIMARY | +1 SECONDARY (week 5+) | PRIMARY first 4 weeks, then add SECONDARY |
| **<10 hours/week** | Part-time GTM | SECONDARY only | Recommend Consulting-First or Dream 100 (lower weekly load) |

**Motion Load Model (Weekly Hours Required):**

| Motion | Hours/Week | Intensity | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Waitlist Heat | 6 | Medium | 2 landings, 2 webinars, email sequences |
| Build-in-Public | 4 | Low | 3 posts/week, light engagement |
| Authority Education | 8 | High | 2 content pieces/week, prep time |
| Wave Riding | 3 | Low | Monitor trends, ship fast, amplify |
| LTD | 5 | Medium | Build funnel, respond to customers |
| Signal Sniper | 8 | High | Daily outreach, sales conversations, CRM |
| Outcome Demo | 5 | Medium | 5-10 demos/week, refinement |
| Hammering | 6 | Medium | Monitor demand, ship features, support |
| BOFU SEO | 6 | Medium | Content research, writing, SEO setup |
| Dream 100 | 5 | Medium | Research, outreach, relationship nurture |
| 7x4x11 | 10 | High | Full-time sales execution, sequences |
| Value Trust | 7 | Medium | Free resource creation, email nurture |
| Paid VSL | 8 | High | VSL creation, ad management, optimization |

**Calculation Logic:**

1. Identify founder's available weekly hours (from assessment or SC4)
2. Look up top 3 recommended PRIMARY/SECONDARY motions
3. Sum hours required for top 1 PRIMARY + best SECONDARY candidate
4. If sum > available hours: recommend descoping to PRIMARY only or switching to lower-load secondary
5. Flag in output if founder is over-committing

**Output (added to gtm-fitness.md):**

If founder is over-committing:
```
⚠️ BANDWIDTH ALERT: Overcommitment Risk

Your top 2 recommended motions require 14 hours/week combined.
You've indicated 10 hours/week availability.

RECOMMENDATION: Launch PRIMARY motion first (6 weeks) before adding SECONDARY.
Or: Swap SECONDARY to Wave Riding (only 3 hours/week).

Over-committing is the #2 failure mode (after inaction). Don't do it.
```

**Impact:**
- Prevents overcommitment that leads to motion abandonment
- Sequences PRIMARY → SECONDARY with realistic timeline
- Flags when founder needs to deprioritize or defer secondary motion

## Gate 4: Pattern Interrupt Readiness (Brendan Kane Hook Framework)

**Applied to:** Authority Education, Build-in-Public, Wave Riding when scored as PRIMARY or SECONDARY.

**Why:** Content motions fail when content is boring. Brendan Kane's framework identifies if planned content has enough "hook" to stop scrollers.

**The 5 Hook Point Formulas:**

1. **Contrarian Take** — Challenges conventional wisdom
   - Example: "Everything you know about email subject lines is wrong"
   - Signal: Founder's positioning includes a contrarian angle

2. **Data Disruption** — Surprising stats or research findings
   - Example: "93% of founders quit before profitability — here's why"
   - Signal: Founder has data, research, or unique findings to share

3. **Behind-the-Curtain** — Insider perspective or "secret" insights
   - Example: "What Stripe doesn't want you to know about payment processing"
   - Signal: Founder has insider knowledge or access

4. **Challenge/Dare** — Actionable challenge to viewer
   - Example: "Do this 1 thing for 7 days, watch what happens"
   - Signal: Founder's motion includes a challenge or experiment element

5. **Story Hook** — Personal narrative that builds curiosity
   - Example: "The $50K mistake that forced me to build this"
   - Signal: Founder has a compelling origin story

**Evaluation (During Assessment):**

Ask founder describing planned content:
- "What's the hook of your first content piece?"
- "Does it challenge convention, surprise with data, reveal insider knowledge, challenge the viewer, or tell a story?"

**Scoring:**

| Hook Elements Present | Readiness Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **3-5 hooks in planned content** | GREEN — Proceed | Content will perform. Launch as planned. |
| **1-2 hooks** | YELLOW — Medium | Content is functional but not compelling. Add hook training. |
| **0 hooks** | RED — At Risk | Content is educational but boring. Will underperform. |

**Output (added to gtm-fitness.md):**

If hooks missing:
```
⚠️ PATTERN INTERRUPT READINESS: Low

Your planned content is educational but lacks hooks. Without pattern interrupt,
content will underperform on social platforms.

RECOMMENDATION: Before launching, spend 2 hours on Pattern Interrupt training.

Hook Formula to Add: [Recommend 1-2 from list above based on founder's strengths]

This will increase content viral potential by 3-5x.
```

**Impact:**
- Identifies boring content before launch
- Adds 2-hour hook training prerequisite if needed
- Dramatically improves performance of content motions

## Gate 5: Engagement Audit (Nir Eyal Hook Model)

**Applied to:** Top 3 recommended motions (PRIMARY + top 2 SECONDARY).

**Why:** Motions fail when they don't create sustained engagement. Nir Eyal's Hook Model evaluates if a motion has enough "stickiness" to convert.

**The Hook Model: 4 Components Per Motion**

1. **Trigger** (External or Internal)
   - External: Email, notification, social post that brings customer back
   - Internal: Habit or curiosity that brings customer back
   - Example for Outcome Demo: Email with "updated demo + 3 new features"

2. **Action** (Minimal Next Step)
   - What's the smallest action to engage with your motion?
   - Example for Build-in-Public: Follow on Twitter, react to post, subscribe
   - Should be <10 seconds

3. **Reward** (Variable, Not Predictable)
   - What's the payoff for taking action?
   - Variability is key: not same reward every time
   - Example for Value Trust: Free resource, new insight, community proof

4. **Investment** (Deepens Engagement)
   - What deepens the customer's commitment over time?
   - Example for Authority Education: Progress through course, access community, get certificate

**Evaluation Checklist (Per Motion):**

For each motion, assess:
- [ ] Clear external trigger that brings customer back
- [ ] Action is simple (<10 seconds)
- [ ] Reward is valuable and somewhat unpredictable
- [ ] Investment path exists (how does customer deepen engagement)

**Scoring:**

| Components Present | Loop Strength | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **4/4 components** | STRONG | Loop is complete. Motion will sustain engagement. Proceed. |
| **3/4 components** | MEDIUM | Loop exists but missing 1 element. Add missing element before launch. |
| **1-2/4 components** | WEAK | Loop is incomplete. Prioritize this motion lower or redesign. |
| **0/4 components** | BROKEN | No engagement loop. Deprioritize or skip. |

**Examples:**

**Outcome Demo (Full Loop):**
- Trigger: Email "Try live demo + get personalized recommendation"
- Action: Schedule 15-min demo call
- Reward: Live walkthrough + custom recommendations (variable each demo)
- Investment: Post-demo access to case study library + community Slack

**Build-in-Public (Full Loop):**
- Trigger: Daily Twitter notification "new update from [founder]"
- Action: React/retweet/reply (5 seconds)
- Reward: See different type of update each day (build log, lesson, win, question)
- Investment: Email signup for early access, eventual paid product access

**Authority Education (Weak Loop):**
- Trigger: Email to list "new guide available"
- Action: Click and read (5 minutes)
- Reward: Learning + framework ← PREDICTABLE, same every time
- Investment: ❌ Missing — no deepening engagement path (no community, no progression)
- **FIX:** Add investment via course progression, community access, or certification

**Output (added to gtm-fitness.md):**

If loop is weak:
```
ENGAGEMENT LOOP AUDIT: [Motion Name]

Your [motion] has 2/4 hook elements. Engagement will drop after initial contact.

Missing Component: [Investment path / Variable rewards / Clear action]

RECOMMENDATION: Add [specific fix] before launch.

This will increase retention and conversion by 2-3x.
```

**Impact:**
- Identifies engagement loops that will fail
- Adds design requirements before launch
- Increases motion sustainability and ROI

## When to Apply Gates (Decision Tree)

```
IF Pattern of Inaction detected (idea >6 months, 0 launches):
    → Apply Gate 1 (72-Hour Rule)

IF Content-dependent motion (Authority, Build-Public, Wave Riding, BOFU, Value Trust) = PRIMARY:
    → Apply Gate 2 (Content Systems Check)

FOR ALL motions:
    → Apply Gate 3 (Bandwidth Calculator)
    → Apply Gate 4 (Pattern Interrupt) if content motion PRIMARY
    → Apply Gate 5 (Engagement Audit) for top 3 motions

IF founder disagrees with recommendation:
    → Re-run expert gates to understand root cause
    → Often reveals missing prerequisite or infrastructure gap
```
