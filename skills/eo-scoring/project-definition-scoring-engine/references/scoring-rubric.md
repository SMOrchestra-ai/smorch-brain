# Detailed Scoring Rubric

Complete evaluation criteria and AI logic for all 21 questions.

---

## UNIVERSAL FREE-TEXT RUBRIC

Every free-text question uses a 0-5 point scale (or variant: 0-6, 0-10) with consistent criteria:

| Score | Label | Definition |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| **0** | Blank/Irrelevant | No answer provided or completely off-topic |
| **1** | Generic | Could apply to any business; no specificity; vague assertions |
| **2** | Directional | Right direction but vague; missing names, numbers, evidence; statements without support |
| **3** | Specific | Named entities, some numbers, clear direction; usable but not sharp; could be sharper |
| **4** | Sharp | Specific, evidence-backed, internally consistent; would guide action; could be used in materials |
| **5** | Expert | Could be used as-is in pitch deck, campaign, or spec; vivid, memorable, actionable |
| **6-10** | Variants | Some questions have extended scales (D3 goes to 5, D2 to 6, B3 to 10); same logic applies with finer gradation |

### Three-Axis Evaluation Framework (All Free-Text)

Every free-text answer is evaluated on three axes:

#### 1. SPECIFICITY — Real, named things vs. generic platitudes

**Score 1 (Generic):**
- "Business owners"
- "Entrepreneurs have problems"
- "There's a gap in the market"

**Score 3 (Specific, but not sharp):**
- "Real estate brokers"
- "Brokers lose leads"
- "Sales teams struggle with organization"

**Score 5 (Expert, highly specific):**
- "Independent real estate brokers in Dubai managing 10-30 listings"
- "Lose 35%+ of leads daily to WhatsApp message chaos"
- "Solo commercial real estate brokers in UAE who rely entirely on WhatsApp for client communication"

#### 2. EVIDENCE QUALITY — Real-world data, conversations, research vs. assumptions

**Score 1 (Assumption):**
- "I think they have this problem"
- "It seems like a big issue"
- "People probably struggle with this"

**Score 3 (Some evidence, specifics):**
- "Talked to 3 brokers who mentioned they use multiple tools"
- "Observed WhatsApp group with lots of messages"
- "Competitor X charges $300/month"

**Score 5 (Multiple evidence types, specifics):**
- "5 broker interviews revealed they lose 40% of leads; I observed WhatsApp group with 200+ untracked messages; competitor X charges $300/month and has 0 solo broker users; industry report shows 15,000 brokers in UAE"

#### 3. INTERNAL CONSISTENCY — Alignment with previous answers and logic

**Score 1 (Contradictory/Illogical):**
- Earlier: "My niche is solo brokers" → Later: "I'm targeting enterprise companies"
- Earlier: "Speed to value is same day" → Later: "MVP requires 15 features"
- No logical flow between clauses

**Score 3 (Generally consistent, one claim unsupported):**
- Mostly aligned answers but one claim seems questionable
- Logic flows but misses connection in one place
- Some circular reasoning

**Score 5 (All claims align, complementary evidence, coherent narrative):**
- Every statement builds on previous ones
- Evidence is complementary (A1 + A3 reinforce each other)
- Narrative has clear arc: problem → why me → why now → what's built

## QUESTION-SPECIFIC SCORING LOGIC

### SECTION A: FOUNDER CONTEXT & PROBLEM ORIGIN

#### A1. Problem Origin Story (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Evidence Quality (60%) > Specificity (30%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Generic market observation with no specificity
- 2: Some specificity, vague detail, no cost articulation
- 3: Specific moment with some detail, lacks emotional clarity or cost
- 4: Vivid moment, named people, timing, some cost ("Q3 2024, my colleague lost...")
- 5: Expert: Specific moment, named people/companies, exact timing, clear cost of inaction, emotional resonance

**Bonus/Penalty:**
- +1 if answer includes quantified financial cost (AED amount, timeline impact, named person affected)
- +1 if reads like a natural VSL opening (compelling, story-driven)
- -1 if "I realized" without specific trigger event
- -1 if generic pain without real moment

**Common Scoring Examples:**

| Answer | Score | Why |
|--------|-------|-----|
| "I realized there's a gap in the market for real estate tools" | 1 | Generic observation, no moment, no specificity |
| "In my previous job, people struggled with lead management" | 2 | Some context, but vague details, no emotional weight |
| "In Q3 2024, my team of 8 brokers lost about 40% of their leads because we were using WhatsApp to communicate and had no way to track conversations" | 4 | Vivid, specific timing, named impact (40%), clear mechanism |
| "In Q3 2024, I watched my mentor, a veteran Dubai broker managing 20 listings, lose a AED 180K deal because he forgot to follow up on an inquiry from 3 days prior. It was buried in a WhatsApp group. That moment, I knew this was worth solving." | 5 | Specific person, exact amount, exact timeline, emotional moment, named location |

---

#### A2. Founder-Problem Fit (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Evidence Quality (50%) > Specificity (40%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Generic passion ("I'm passionate about entrepreneurship") with no unique advantage
- 2: Some relevant experience, no clear advantage ("I worked in real estate for 2 years")
- 3: Clear relevant experience with some indication of unfair advantage, not fully developed
- 4: Specific advantage with evidence ("I managed 50+ brokers at XYZ, know their exact workflow")
- 5: Crystal-clear unfair advantage ("I spent 8 years as a broker, own network of 200+ brokers in MENA, 1,000+ conversations")

**Bonus/Penalty:**
- +1 if advantage would take competitor 2+ years to replicate
- +1 if includes specific network size or access detail
- -1 if "I know the problem intimately" without detail
- -1 if duration/scope not named

**Critical Check:** "Why CAN'T a smart outsider just copy what you're building?" If the answer is "Because I have [specific advantage]," that's 4-5. If the answer is "Umm...", that's 1-2.

---

#### A3. Problem Validation Evidence (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Evidence Quality (70%) > Specificity (20%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: No evidence beyond founder's own assumption ("I know this is a problem")
- 2: One evidence type mentioned vaguely ("I talked to people about it") with no specifics
- 3: 1-2 evidence types with specifics (e.g., "Talked to 5 brokers; they all use WhatsApp + spreadsheets")
- 4: 2-3 evidence types with detail (conversations + competitor pricing + industry data)
- 5: Multiple evidence types with specifics (5+ buyer conversations with quotes + 2 failed competitors analyzed + industry growth data + hiring trends)

**Bonus/Penalty:**
- +1 if market data / competitor research included with specifics
- +1 if evidence is recent (last 90 days)
- -1 if "They said yes, this is a problem" without behavioral evidence

**What Counts as Evidence:**
- Good: "5 brokers independently mentioned they use WhatsApp + Google Sheets + phone calls"
- Better: "5 brokers showed me their WhatsApp groups with 100+ untracked messages"
- Best: "5 brokers in [named locations], I recorded conversations where they quantified the cost (AED X lost), AND I observed competitor X charges $300/month with zero solo broker signups"

### SECTION B: 3-LEVEL NICHE DEFINITION

#### B1. Market Level (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Specificity (60%) > Evidence Quality (30%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Too vague ("Technology," "Software") OR too narrow for a market ("WhatsApp bots")
- 2: Overly broad with vague boundaries ("Business software") OR too narrow
- 3: Recognizable market with some definition ("Real Estate Technology") but could be sharper
- 4: Clear market category with natural boundaries ("CRM software for real estate professionals")
- 5: Expert: Clear market, defined scope, multiple clear competitors named ("Real Estate Technology — CRM, lead capture, and deal management tools for real estate professionals")

**Market-Sizing Check:** Market should allow for 10+ competitors. If student can't name 3-4 competitors in their defined market, the market is too narrow.

---

#### B2. Sub-Market Level (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Specificity (70%) > Evidence Quality (20%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Same as market (no narrowing) OR still very broad
- 2: Some narrowing but vague ("CRM for small brokers")
- 3: Clear sub-market with identifiable characteristics ("CRM for independent real estate brokers managing <50 listings")
- 4: Sharp sub-market that clearly narrows B1 ("WhatsApp-integrated lead capture for independent real estate brokers in Gulf countries managing 10-50 active listings")
- 5: Expert: So specific you could create a Facebook ad targeting this exact group

**Sub-Market-Sizing Check:** Sub-market should be 10-50% the size of the market. If you said "market is 100,000 professionals," sub-market should be 10,000-50,000.

**Specificity Ladder:**
- "CRM for brokers" → 1 (same as market)
- "CRM for small brokers" → 2 (still vague)
- "CRM for independent brokers in real estate" → 3 (specific, but not sharp)
- "WhatsApp-native CRM for independent real estate brokers managing 10-50 listings in MENA" → 4-5 (actionable, ad-targetable)

---

#### B3. Niche Level (0-10 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Specificity (60%) > Evidence Quality (25%) > Consistency (15%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1-2: Missing 3-4 elements (just "brokers" or just "lead tracking")
- 3-4: Missing 1-2 elements; partial niche
- 5-6: All 4 elements present, but one underdeveloped OR moderate specificity
- 7-8: Sharp niche with all 4 elements + demonstrates research (niche behaviors, tool stack, competitor knowledge)
- 9-10: World-class: Precise niche that reads like case study opening; includes outcome metric and timeline

**Four-Element Checklist (MUST all be present for score >3):**
1. Specific Person: "Independent brokers" (not "real estate professionals")
2. Specific Situation: "Managing 10-30 listings, all communication in WhatsApp"
3. Specific Problem: "Lose 40%+ of leads due to manual tracking"
4. Specific Outcome: "Automated capture + follow-up without switching tools"

**Specificity Ladder:**

| Example | Score | Why |
|---------|-------|-----|
| "Brokers" | 1 | Only 1 element |
| "Real estate brokers who lose leads" | 3 | 2 elements (person + problem), missing situation and outcome |
| "Independent brokers in Dubai managing 10-30 listings who lose leads to WhatsApp chaos" | 5 | All 4 elements present, but "loses leads to chaos" is vague |
| "Independent brokers in Dubai managing 10-30 active listings lose 35%+ of leads daily because all communication happens in WhatsApp groups with no follow-up system; we solve this by capturing every lead automatically inside WhatsApp" | 7-8 | All 4 elements sharp, includes behavioral detail and mechanism |
| "The 2,000 independent brokers in Dubai and Abu Dhabi, each managing 15-40 active listings, who lose an average of 5-8 deals/month (worth AED 20-40K each) because they track leads across WhatsApp, email, and SMS with zero centralization—resulting in the broker texting the lead, forgetting to follow up, and the deal going cold." | 9-10 | All 4 elements, includes TAM sizing, outcome metric (deals lost), financial impact, named locations |

---

#### B4. Niche Validation Logic (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Evidence Quality (60%) > Specificity (30%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: No reasoning beyond assertion ("It's my passion")
- 2: Basic logic without economic reasoning ("This group has pain")
- 3: Some reasoning including market dynamics, but vague ("Bigger companies have better tools; this group doesn't")
- 4: Clear reasoning including addressable size trade-off, competitive advantage, pricing power, and one expansion path
- 5: Sophisticated economic reasoning (why underserved, why competitors ignore, why pricing works, how to expand sequentially)

**Required Elements (for 4-5):**
- Why would you lose revenue/traction if you went broader?
- What competitive advantage disappears if you serve the whole sub-market?
- Why can you charge more (or charge at all) in this niche?
- What's the expansion path if you dominate this niche first?

**Scoring Examples:**

| Answer | Score | Why |
|--------|-------|-----|
| "This is the best market." | 1 | Pure assertion |
| "This group has pain." | 2 | Basic, no economic reasoning |
| "Bigger companies like HubSpot target enterprise; solo brokers are ignored." | 3 | Directional, but vague on YOUR advantage |
| "If we served all brokers globally, we'd compete with Pipedrive/HubSpot (10x resources). Solo MENA brokers have unique needs: WhatsApp-native preference, Arabic UI, local payment methods. We can charge AED 100/month because their alternative (HubSpot) is AED 1000/month and requires desktop work. Expansion: after dominating solo brokers in UAE, we expand to boutique brokerages (5-10 person teams), then franchise brokers." | 4-5 | Economics clear, competitive advantage specific, expansion path defined |

---

#### B5. Niche Size Estimation (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Evidence Quality (70%) > Specificity (20%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Pure guess with no reasoning ("I think there's 1,000")
- 2: Some methodology but weak ("There are brokers in Dubai") without numbers
- 3: Estimation attempt with partial methodology ("Dubai has X buildings...") but gaps in logic
- 4: Solid bottom-up: "UAE real estate board has 15,000 brokers; 60% are independent; 40% manage 10-30 listings = ~3,600"
- 5: Detailed with geography breakdown, named sources, realistic adjustments, multiple validation methods

**Bonus/Penalty:**
- +1 if multiple geography references (UAE, KSA, Egypt breakdowns)
- +1 if margin of error acknowledged ("±20%")
- -1 if number without any math

**Methodology Examples:**

| Approach | Score |
|----------|-------|
| "I think there are 1,000 brokers in Dubai" | 1 | Pure guess
| "There are real estate professionals everywhere" | 2 | No numbers
| "According to industry reports, there are 15,000 real estate professionals in UAE" | 3 | Number + source, but not narrowed to niche
| "UAE real estate authority lists 15,000 licensed brokers. 70% are independent (10,500). Of those, 50% manage 10-50 listings (5,250). These are my niche." | 4 | Bottom-up with clear math
| "UAE: 15,000 brokers, 70% independent, 50% manage 10-50 listings = 5,250. KSA: 8,000 brokers, 60% independent, 50% = 2,400. Total MENA TAM: ~8,000 brokers (conservative, ±25% margin of error). In year 1, targeting UAE only: 5,250." | 5 | Multiple geographies, realistic, includes margin of error |

### SECTION C: POSITIONING & DIFFERENTIATION

#### C1. Category Definition (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Specificity (70%) > Evidence Quality (20%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Missing one or both blanks, or both vague ("...a SaaS for business people")
- 2: Both filled but vague ("...a tool for professionals")
- 3: Both filled, somewhat specific ("...a platform for managing real estate")
- 4: Both filled, specific and clear ("...a WhatsApp CRM for independent brokers")
- 5: Crisp, memorable, could use in pitch deck ("...a WhatsApp-first lead system for independent Gulf brokers managing teams under 5")

**Blank Quality Check:**
- First blank (HOW): Describes feature + channel (WhatsApp-based, AI-powered, mobile-first, no-code, relationship-engine)
- Second blank (WHO): Describes niche, NOT market (small brokers, freelance designers, female founders, NOT "professionals")

---

#### C2. Competitive Alternatives (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Evidence Quality (70%) > Specificity (20%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Can't name alternatives OR only direct competitors without buyer behavior
- 2: Names 1-2 alternatives, doesn't articulate what's broken
- 3: Names 3 alternatives with surface-level weakness ("It's too expensive," "It's slow")
- 4: Names 3 alternatives with specific failure points ("Leads fall through the cracks; I lose AED 5K deals")
- 5: Expert: Names 3 real alternatives (including status quo), explains specific failure for each, shows understanding of buyer lock-in

**What Counts as "Alternatives":**
- Current behavior (doing nothing, manual process)
- Competitive tools (HubSpot, Pipedrive)
- Workarounds (spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups)

**What Counts as "What's Broken":**
- Feature gap: "No way to track who said what"
- Friction: "Requires desktop work; brokers only have mobile"
- Cost: "AED 1,000/month; too expensive for solo broker"
- Emotional: "I lose deals because I forget to follow up"

**Bad Answer:** "HubSpot and Pipedrive are competitors." (Just names, no buyer behavior)
**Good Answer:** "Today, brokers use: (1) WhatsApp groups (broken: no history, no automation); (2) Google Sheets (broken: manual updates, lost leads); (3) HubSpot (broken: AED 1000/month, overkill, requires desktop)"

---

#### C3. Unique Mechanism (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Specificity (70%) > Evidence Quality (20%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Feature list ("We have mobile, automation, reporting")
- 2: Mechanism-adjacent but generic ("We're mobile-first," "We're easier")
- 3: Real mechanism but not unique ("We automate lead tracking")
- 4: Specific mechanism that differentiates ("WhatsApp-native instead of forcing tool switching")
- 5: Ownable mechanism competitors can't easily claim ("Passive SMS follow-up triggered by lead timestamp, requiring zero broker action")

**Feature vs. Mechanism:**
- Feature: "We have a dashboard"
- Mechanism: "We show results inside WhatsApp, so brokers never leave the app they live in"

- Feature: "We have automation"
- Mechanism: "We auto-follow-up without the broker touching anything, based on lead age + inbox status"

**Defensibility Check:**
- Would take competitor 6+ months to replicate? → 4-5
- Makes the old way OBSOLETE? (not just "better") → 5
- Ownable (can you defend this in pitch?) → 4-5

---

#### C4. One-Line Wedge (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Specificity (60%) > Evidence Quality (30%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Generic marketing speak ("Transform your business," "The future is here")
- 2: Specific but no curiosity gap ("Real estate CRM for brokers")
- 3: Creates some interest but not specific ("Lose fewer leads with better tools")
- 4: Specific and creates curiosity gap ("The WhatsApp CRM that auto-follows up while you close")
- 5: Expert: Would genuinely stop the scroll — specific problem + intriguing solution + niche call-out

**Wedge Anatomy (all three must be present for 4-5):**
1. **Mentions the niche:** (Not generic "professionals")
2. **Creates a gap:** Problem → implied solution
3. **Is clickable:** Would you click this on your own feed?

**Examples:**

| Answer | Score | Why |
|--------|-------|-----|
| "Better lead management for real estate" | 2 | Specific but no gap |
| "Stop losing 40% of your leads to WhatsApp chaos — capture and auto-follow up without leaving the app" | 4 | Problem + solution + niche (implied: brokers using WhatsApp) |
| "You're losing AED 5-10K deals to WhatsApp chaos. We capture every lead and auto-follow-up inside WhatsApp—zero tool-switching." | 5 | Financial cost + problem + mechanism + niche call-out, would stop scroll |

### SECTION D: PRODUCT VISION & SPEC

#### D1. Core Problem Statement (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Specificity (60%) > Evidence Quality (30%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Abstract problem without workflow ("Lead management is hard")
- 2: Some workflow, vague failure points ("Brokers lose track of leads")
- 3: Clear workflow with one failure point ("They get leads, they forget to follow up")
- 4: Step-by-step workflow with multiple named breakdowns ("Get lead → WhatsApp → no notes → forget context → lose deal")
- 5: Expert: Step-by-step with emotional/financial impact ("Day 1: 10 leads via WhatsApp... Day 3: lead#7 calls back, broker has no context, loses deal to competitor")

**Workflow Must Include:**
- Current tools/processes (WhatsApp, Sheets, phone calls, etc.)
- Step-by-step sequence
- Specific failure point(s)
- If possible, cost of failure

---

#### D2. MVP Feature Set (0-6 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Specificity (60%) > Evidence Quality (30%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Feature dump (10+ features, no problem linkage)
- 2: 4-5 features with minimal problem-linkage explanation
- 3: 3-5 features with some problem mapping, but justifications are generic
- 4: 3-5 features with clear problem-to-feature mapping and reasonable "why can't cut" reasoning
- 5: Tight MVP with crystal-clear mapping, no superfluous features, "can't cut" logic is specific
- 6: Expert: 3-5 features, clear mapping, "can't cut" includes sequencing logic ("Feature 1 enables Feature 2; Feature 2 without Feature 1 loses impact")

**Feature Count Rules:**
- <3 features → too vague, max 3
- 3-5 features → optimal
- 6+ features → not MVP, max 2

**Problem-Feature Mapping Check:**

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "Dashboard, Mobile app, Reporting, Integrations" | "Lead Auto-Capture from WhatsApp: Solves 'broker gets 10 leads daily but forgets context.' Can't cut because without this, broker still manually entering data." |
| "Nice-to-haves that can be cut" | "Core features linked directly to D1 workflow breakdown" |
| "Features from competitors" | "Features that solve YOUR specific problem" |

---

#### D3. Speed to Value (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Evidence Quality (50%) > Specificity (40%) > Consistency (10%)

**Hybrid Scoring (MC base + Free-text adjustment):**

**MC Base Score:**
- Same day: 5
- Within 3 days: 4
- Within a week: 3
- Within a month: 2
- Longer than a month: 1

**Free-text Adjustment (max ±1):**
- Vague first result ("They'll see value") → -1
- Specific first result ("They capture 10 leads, see them grouped, hit SMS button to auto-follow") → +0 to +1

**Final Score:** MC base + free-text adjustment (cap at 5)

**First Result Specificity:**

| Vague | Specific |
|-------|----------|
| "They'll solve the problem" | "They'll capture their first 10 leads from WhatsApp in the app, see them grouped by status, and hit 'follow-up reminder' to SMS all cold leads at once—all in <30 min" |
| "They'll see value" | "Day 1, Morning: Broker installs app, logs in, syncs WhatsApp. Within 10 min, app shows 20 recent WhatsApp leads they'd been tracking in notes. Day 1, Evening: Broker closes first deal using lead intel the app surfaced." |

#### D4. Technical Approach (0-6 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Specificity (80%) > Evidence Quality (15%) > Consistency (5%)

**Base Score:**
- No-code: 6
- Hybrid: 5
- Low-code: 4
- Custom code: 3
- Haven't decided: 1

**No Further Adjustment (direct assignment from MC option)**

**MicroSaaS Context Rationale:**
- No-code scores highest because: fastest build, cheapest, best margins, easiest pivot
- Custom code only scores high if student has clear CTO + realistic timeline
- "Haven't decided" signals low execution conviction

**Follow-Up Question (for custom code or haven't decided):**
"Why custom code? Do you have a co-founder/CTO? What's the timeline?"

#### E2. Origin Story (0-7 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Evidence Quality (60%) > Specificity (30%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Resume-like (worked here, worked there, interested in tech)
- 2: Some personal element, missing emotional clarity
- 3: Clear personal story with some emotion, lacks urgency
- 4: Specific story with emotional moment and clear connection to product
- 5: Expert story: Specific moment, named impact (financial/emotional), tried and failed alternatives, clear why-you-now

**Required Elements (for 4+):**

- EMOTIONAL RESONANCE (not just logical)
- CONNECTION to the product you're building

**Bonus Triggers:**

- +1 if includes quantified loss (AED amount, deals lost, time wasted)
- +1 if names the failed alternative (tried HubSpot, didn't work, building alternative)

#### F3. Cultural Fit Assessment (0-5 scale)

**Scoring Axis Priority:** Specificity (70%) > Evidence Quality (20%) > Consistency (10%)

**Base Score Determination:**
- 0: No answer
- 1: Acknowledges MENA culture but no product adaptation ("MENA is different")
- 2: Acknowledges 1 cultural factor with vague adaptation ("We're WhatsApp-friendly")
- 3: Acknowledges 2+ cultural factors with specific adaptation for 1 ("We stay in WhatsApp because that's how MENA business works; we also support local payment methods")
- 4: Addresses 2+ factors with specific product/service adaptations ("WhatsApp-native; Arabic UI; flexible payment [card + bank transfer]; Ramadan-aware")
- 5: Expert: Multiple cultural adaptations with evidence of research ("We observed X, so we built Y; we researched Z, so we adapted...")

**Cultural Factors (choose 2+):**
- Relationship-based selling (vs. transactional)
- WhatsApp preference
- Ramadan seasonality
- Trust-first buying
- Payment infrastructure (local vs. credit card)
- Decision-making styles (family business, consensus)
- Family business dynamics

**Bonus/Penalty:**
- +1 if Ramadan, payment infrastructure, OR Arabic/relationship factors specifically mentioned
- +1 if answer shows primary research (conversations with MENA users, observations)
- -1 if just lists cultural factors without showing product adaptation

**Examples:**

| Answer | Score |
|--------|-------|
| "MENA is relationship-focused." | 1 | Just acknowledgment, no adaptation |
| "We're WhatsApp-friendly." | 2 | One factor, vague adaptation |
| "We stay in WhatsApp because MENA uses WhatsApp for business; we support local payment like bank transfer because credit cards aren't universal." | 3 | Two factors, specific adaptation |
| "WhatsApp-native for communication preference; Arabic UI for Arab-first users; flexible payment (card + bank transfer); Ramadan-aware (let brokers pause reminders during fasting hours); relationship engine (SMS + voice in Arabic, named by preference)." | 4-5 | Multiple specific adaptations, evidence of research |

## MENA-SPECIFIC SCORING ADJUSTMENTS (Applied During Phase 2)

These are bonuses applied during scoring, not new questions:

### 1. Niche Geography Bonus (Section B, B3)
- If B3 includes MENA geography (UAE, KSA, Egypt, Gulf state) → +1 to B3
- If B3 references MENA-specific pain (WhatsApp-centric, relationship-based, local payment issues) → +1 to B3

### 2. Problem Origin MENA Ground (Section A, A1 & A2)
- If A1 includes named MENA people, companies, or locations → +1 to A1
- If A2 includes MENA network or MENA domain experience → +1 to A2

### 3. Language-Geography Alignment (Section F, F2)
- If F2 = "Arabic-first" AND F1 = Egypt/KSA → base score 4 (not 3)
- If F2 = "English-only" AND F1 = Egypt → recommendation flag only (no score penalty)

### 4. Cultural Fit Research Bonus (Section F, F3)
- If F3 demonstrates primary research (spoke to MENA operators) → +1 to F3
