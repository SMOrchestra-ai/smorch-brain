## 8 FOUNDER ARCHETYPES

### 1. LAUNCHER (Ambitious + Resources)

**Profile:**
- High capital availability ($10K+)
- Full-time commitment (40+ hrs/week)
- Aggressive risk tolerance
- Moderate to strong execution track record

**Primary Strategy Path:** Replicate & Localize
- Resources enable adaptation costs
- Ambition attracts global partners
- Timeline tolerance supports longer builds

**Attractive Character Archetype:** REPORTER
- Brand voice: "I discovered something working elsewhere; let me bring it here"
- Narrative: Credibility through proven model validation; introducing tested solution to new market
- Positioning: You're the explorer/adapter; you validate what works globally, then localize fearlessly

**30-Day Experiment:** Validate local pain exists for global product (10 customer interviews + landing page)

**Characteristics:**
- Strong decision-making ability
- Comfortable with capital allocation
- Network-oriented (can secure partnerships)
- Risk-aware but not risk-averse

**Potential Pitfalls:**
- Over-investment in non-core activities
- Perfectionism delaying launch
- Rapid pivots without sufficient validation

**Coaching Focus:**
- Validate before scaling spend
- Set clear success criteria before capital deployment
- Track ROI per dollar spent

### 2. MARKET HUNTER (Sales-Focused)

**Profile:**
- Strong sales/relationships skill
- Mid-range capital ($2K-10K)
- Part-time to full-time commitment
- Moderate risk tolerance
- Existing network in target market

**Primary Strategy Path:** Consulting-First SaaS
- Sales skills translate to consulting closes
- Network generates first clients
- Fast revenue validates demand
- Consulting client feedback guides SaaS build

**Attractive Character Archetype:** LEADER
- Brand voice: "I've solved this at scale; follow my system"
- Narrative: Authority through results; you position as expert who refined process across many clients
- Positioning: You're the authority; you've worked with multiple buyers, so you know the real problem

**30-Day Experiment:** Close 3 consulting clients in 30 days via warm network (50 outreach + 15 conversations)

**Characteristics:**
- Relationship-building natural
- Comfortable with direct outreach
- Can articulate customer pain clearly
- Prefer people-facing work

**Potential Pitfalls:**
- Distraction (trying too many prospects)
- Underselling expertise (pricing too low)
- Difficulty transitioning from service to product

**Coaching Focus:**
- Raise pricing (expert pricing, not discounted)
- Document systemically as you consult (feeds SaaS roadmap)
- Set defined transition timeline from consulting → SaaS
- Protect time for productization alongside consulting delivery

---

### 3. CHANNEL SEEKER (Distribution Advantage)

**Profile:**
- Existing audience or distribution channel
- Technical capability (medium to high)
- Variable capital (depends on channel)
- Product-delivery focused
- Clear acquisition channel already exists

**Primary Strategy Path:** Boring Micro-SaaS
- Leverage existing distribution
- Fast launch enabled by channel advantage
- Product-led growth natural
- Rapid iteration loop possible

**Attractive Character Archetype:** RELUCTANT HERO
- Brand voice: "I never wanted to build this, but the market needed it"
- Narrative: Humility through necessity; solving real problem that frustrated you enough to act
- Positioning: You're solving a specific pain for your audience; you've seen the need firsthand

**30-Day Experiment:** Launch landing page + MVP; get 5 pre-pays in 14 days from existing audience

**Characteristics:**
- Already has audience/distribution
- Strong product intuition
- Speed-oriented
- Lean operator

**Potential Pitfalls:**
- Assuming product-channel fit when it may not exist
- Scaling acquisition without optimization
- Building features customers don't need

**Coaching Focus:**
- Validate that channel audience actually has pain you're solving
- Measure CAC vs. LTV meticulously
- Resist feature bloat; stay boring
- Test paid acquisition alongside organic

---

### 4. SOLUTION SEEKER (Product-Focused)

**Profile:**
- Strong technical or product capability
- Limited capital ($0-5K)
- Part-time to full-time commitment
- Willing to move slower
- Deep domain expertise in niche

**Primary Strategy Path:** Hammering Deep
- Product excellence is unfair advantage
- Niche dominance strategy aligns with product focus
- Deeper moat possible than broad market
- Expertise naturally translates to niche authority

**Attractive Character Archetype:** ADVENTURER
- Brand voice: "I'm going deeper than anyone; come with me"
- Narrative: Exploration through specialization; inviting others into domain where you're guide
- Positioning: You're the specialist; you understand the niche deeply and can guide others through it

**30-Day Experiment:** Publish 10 authority pieces + measure engagement; close 1 pilot customer

**Characteristics:**
- Deep technical/product knowledge
- Less sales-oriented (prefers to build)
- Willing to go narrow first (deep over broad)
- Perfectionist about product quality

**Potential Pitfalls:**
- Over-engineering MVP
- Slow to market
- Difficulty articulating niche-specific positioning
- Resistance to sales/marketing

**Coaching Focus:**
- Constrain MVP scope ruthlessly (boring features only)
- Get to customers sooner (even with "incomplete" product)
- Combine technical excellence with niche authority (content + community)
- Partner with sales-focused co-founder or hire early

### 5. EXPERT WITHOUT A STAGE (Domain Expertise, No Platform)

**Profile:**
- Deep domain expertise (20+ years or expert credibility)
- Strong existing network in domain
- Moderate capital ($2K-10K)
- Part-time or full-time commitment
- No platform or public presence yet
- Often transitioning from corporate role

**Primary Strategy Path:** Consulting-First SaaS (DEFAULT RECOMMENDATION)

**Why this path is the expert's natural fit:**
- They already HAVE the expertise — they don't need to build it
- Consulting generates revenue in 30 days (vs 6+ months for product-first)
- Consulting clients BECOME the product advisory board — they tell you exactly what to build
- The expert's corporate network provides warm leads for consulting (no cold outreach needed for first 3-5 clients)
- Consulting proof → SaaS credibility. "I've solved this for 10 companies manually" is the strongest possible positioning for a SaaS launch

**Attractive Character Archetype:** LEADER or RELUCTANT HERO (depends on communication style)
- **Leader if:** Founder leads with authority, has published work, or explicit credentials
- **Reluctant Hero if:** Founder leads with humility, problem-first framing, or reluctance ("I didn't plan to be the expert, but...")

**30-Day Experiment:** Close 3 consulting clients in 30 days via corporate network (50 warm outreach)

**Expert Archetype Playbook:**

**MONTH 1: Package Expertise as Service**
- Define ONE service offering based on narrowed niche (from SC1)
- Price at $2K-5K/month or $5K-15K/project (NOT $500 — expert pricing)
- Reach out to 20 warm contacts from corporate network
- Close 2-3 consulting clients
- Deliver manually (this IS the product research phase)

**MONTH 2: Systematize & Document**
- Document every workflow you repeat across clients
- Identify the 3-5 tasks that should be software, not manual
- Start building MVP of the repeatable workflow
- Continue consulting (revenue funds the build)

**MONTH 3: Productize the Pattern**
- Launch MVP to existing consulting clients as "beta" of the SaaS product
- Price SaaS at $200-500/month (consulting clients get lifetime discount)
- Use consulting case studies as proof for SaaS marketing
- Begin GTM motions from SC5 (with proof, credibility, and revenue already in hand)

**Characteristics:**
- Deep credibility and domain knowledge
- Strong professional network
- Often transitioning from big company to startup
- Understands customer pain deeply

**Potential Pitfalls:**
- Impostor syndrome ("Am I really an expert?")
- Difficulty articulating expertise without credentials
- Over-engineering first service offering
- Too much time spent on building product before validating consulting demand

**Coaching Focus:**
- Raise pricing confidence (market will pay for your expertise)
- Start consulting fast (don't wait for perfect positioning or brand)
- Use consulting delivery to clarify product roadmap
- Set defined transition point from consulting → SaaS (Month 4-6, not "someday")
- Build platform alongside consulting (content, speaking, thought leadership) for SaaS launch credibility

**When to OVERRIDE the default (recommend different path):**
- If founder has ZERO corporate network in target geography → Boring Micro-SaaS (need product-led growth)
- If founder's expertise is in a commodity space (no differentiation) → Hammering Deep (niche deeper first)
- If founder explicitly refuses services ("I only want to build product") → Boring Micro-SaaS, but flag: "Building without consulting first means you're guessing what the market wants. Consulting is the fastest validation path for your expertise level."

### 6. GHOST (Stealth, Unknown)

**Profile:**
- No public presence or personal brand
- Limited network in target market
- Technical or product focus
- Lean capital ($0-5K)
- Uncertain about positioning

**Primary Strategy Path:** Boring Micro-SaaS
- Product speaks louder than personal brand
- Can bootstrap with minimal spend
- Product-led growth more viable than consulting (no existing credibility)
- Speed to revenue compensates for unknown status

**Attractive Character Archetype:** RELUCTANT HERO (or stay silent initially)
- Brand voice: "I'm not trying to be famous; I'm just solving a problem that existed for me"
- Narrative: Let the product do the talking; credibility builds as customers succeed
- Positioning: You don't need a stage; the product's value is your stage

**30-Day Experiment:** Build MVP + landing page; get 5 pre-pays in 14 days

**Characteristics:**
- Tend to prefer execution over positioning
- Comfortable working in shadows (stealth builders)
- Focus on product quality over narrative
- Often introverted or privately ambitious

**Potential Pitfalls:**
- Launching to no audience
- Difficulty finding early customers (no network to leverage)
- Product solves for internal use case, not market demand
- Underestimating importance of positioning/narrative

**Coaching Focus:**
- Build product for OTHER people's pain, not just yours
- Start building audience/email list BEFORE launch (content, community participation)
- Lean into "boring, working product" positioning
- Test paid acquisition early (can't rely on organic/network)
- Consider brief "coming out of stealth" announcement at launch (medium post, Product Hunt, LinkedIn)

### 7. OPERATOR (Operations-Focused)

**Profile:**
- Strong operations/process/systematization skills
- Moderate capital ($5K-20K)
- Full-time or part-time commitment
- Systems/efficiency-focused
- Often has experience in ops roles (CoS, VP Ops, etc.)

**Primary Strategy Path:** Replicate & Localize
- Operations skills enable complex localization
- Can systematize replication process
- Strong at execution sequencing
- Can manage partnerships with vendors (key for Replicate path)

**Attractive Character Archetype:** REPORTER
- Brand voice: "I discovered something working elsewhere; let me bring it here and make it work systematically"
- Narrative: You're the systematizer; you take proven models and operationalize them for new markets
- Positioning: You're not the innovator; you're the executor who makes proven models work locally

**30-Day Experiment:** Validate local pain exists + secure partnership + plan localization roadmap

**Characteristics:**
- Process-oriented
- Detail-focused
- Excel at project management
- Comfortable with complexity

**Potential Pitfalls:**
- Over-engineering initial phase (operations can wait)
- Analysis paralysis (wanting perfect process before launch)
- Difficulty pivoting (processes become rigid)
- Team-building expectations (operators often imagine larger teams)

**Coaching Focus:**
- Validate product demand BEFORE systematizing operations
- Keep MVP phase lean (don't build for scale yet)
- Focus on speed-to-market before perfection
- Plan for rapid evolution of processes post-validation
- Recognize that early-stage requires flexibility over systems

### 8. SCRAMBLER (Early-Stage, Figuring It Out)

**Profile:**
- Variable skills (no single strength)
- Very limited capital ($0-1K)
- Part-time commitment (<20 hrs/week)
- High risk tolerance (willing to experiment)
- First-time founder or transitioning roles
- Figuring out what they want to build

**Primary Strategy Path:** Consulting-First SaaS
- Fast revenue validates ANY direction
- Low capital requirement (mostly time)
- Consulting helps figure out what to build
- Revenue provides breathing room for exploration

**Attractive Character Archetype:** RELUCTANT HERO or ADVENTURER (depends on mindset)
- **Reluctant Hero if:** "I stumbled into this and decided to solve it"
- **Adventurer if:** "I'm exploring what my next big thing is"

**30-Day Experiment:** Close 1-2 consulting clients quickly (help with immediate problem; learn the space)

**Characteristics:**
- Scrappy and resourceful
- Fast learners
- Willing to wear many hats
- Often underestimate their own abilities
- Seeking clarity through doing

**Potential Pitfalls:**
- Dilution (trying to do everything at once)
- Burnout (too many hats, limited resources)
- Lack of focus (every idea seems viable)
- Underpricing expertise/services

**Coaching Focus:**
- Pick ONE service to sell (not ten ideas)
- Go find 5 customers willing to pay for that service (validates direction)
- Use consulting revenue to fund next phase (product, education, or different service)
- Resist shiny objects (focus on first traction before pivoting)
- Build confidence through small wins (1-2 paying clients is a big win for scrabler)
- Document learning as you go (becomes future content/positioning)

## PORTFOLIO VS. SINGLE PRODUCT FRAMEWORK

**Context:** John Rush and Marc Lou have both published frameworks for building portfolios of 20+ products as solopreneurs, with compounding revenue across multiple micro-SaaS products or ventures.

**Applicability by Strategy Path:**

For founders selected into **Boring Micro-SaaS** or **Hammering Deep** paths, Claude presents a secondary opportunity assessment.

### PORTFOLIO OPTION: Post-Validation Product Expansion

After validating Product 1 through the 30-day experiment (and reaching initial traction), consider launching Product 2 in parallel IF all three conditions are met:

| Condition | Specification | Evaluation |
|---|---|---|
| **(a) Product 1 is self-serve with minimal maintenance** | Product 1 requires <2 hours/week of your active time (no service delivery, support is automated/community-driven, or outsourced) | Boring Micro-SaaS is ideal here; Hammering Deep requires more founder time initially |
| **(b) Adjacent ICP pain identified in SC2** | During SC2 ICP deep-dive, founder identified 2-3 adjacent pain points the same ICP suffers (not solved by Product 1) | Example: Product 1 solves invoicing; Product 2 opportunity is expense tracking for same ICP |
| **(c) Technical skill allows rapid shipping** | Founder can build MVP for Product 2 in <4 weeks using same tech stack/skills as Product 1 | Requires either founder technical skill or access to co-builder/contractor already on payroll |

**Portfolio Revenue Compounding Example:**
- Month 3: Product 1 generates $500/month (5 customers × $100 MRR)
- Month 6: Product 1 at $1500/month; Product 2 launches at $300/month → $1800 MRR total
- Month 9: Product 1 at $2500/month; Product 2 at $1000/month; Product 3 launches → $3500/month total
- By Month 12: 3 products × moderate ACV = $5000+/month with solo founder (vs. single product plateauing at $2500)

### When to OVERRIDE (recommend single-product focus instead):
- If Product 1 is still <$1000/month MRR → Optimize before expanding; compounding only works if first product is stable
- If Hammering Deep niche is not yet "dominated" (founder is still building authority) → Depth first; breadth later
- If founder is explicitly anti-portfolio ("I want to go deep on ONE product") → Respect preference; note that portfolio approach compounds faster but requires systems/delegation

**AI Processing:** At end of 30-day experiment, if conditions (a), (b), (c) are met and Product 1 shows strong traction, Claude flags "Portfolio Opportunity Detected" and recommends assessment: "You've validated Product 1. You've identified adjacent pain in SC2 (condition b). Do you have the capacity to build Product 2 in parallel? If yes, we can sequence your 90-day roadmap to include Product 2 MVP launch alongside Product 1 optimization."

These examples show the hardest archetype classification decisions — when a founder could map to two different archetypes.

### Borderline: EXPERT WITHOUT A STAGE vs. SOLUTION SEEKER

**Profile A (EXPERT WITHOUT A STAGE):**
- 15 years in commercial real estate, worked at CBRE (global brokerage firm)
- Built systematic lead management process across 500+ brokers
- No public presence, no personal brand, no published work
- Strong network in real estate (200+ professional contacts in GCC)
- Building WhatsApp CRM for brokers

**Profile B (SOLUTION SEEKER):**
- 15 years in software engineering, built systems for 5+ startups
- Deep technical expertise in database optimization and API design
- No published work, no personal brand, no network in real estate
- Building WhatsApp CRM for brokers as first solo venture
- Limited capital ($0-5K), willing to move slowly

**Archetype Decision:**

**Profile A = EXPERT WITHOUT A STAGE** — Recommendation: Consulting-First SaaS
- Reasoning: Deep domain expertise (15 years) + strong network (200 contacts) = immediate consulting opportunity. Can close 3 consulting clients in 30 days with warm network. Consulting revenue funds the build. Product advisory board comes from consulting clients.

**Profile B = SOLUTION SEEKER** — Recommendation: Hammering Deep
- Reasoning: Deep technical capability (5 startups, database/API expertise) but no domain credibility yet. Should go narrow (dominate the niche through product excellence + authority building). Network in real estate is zero, so consulting-first won't work. Boring Micro-SaaS is too risky with zero capital.

**Borderline Case (Profile C):**
- 10 years in commercial real estate, but as an operational analyst, not broker
- Built some process improvements but no significant domain authority
- Published 3 articles on real estate tech challenges
- Moderate network (50-75 contacts in real estate)
- Some technical capability (no-code tools, basic automation)

**Decision Tension:**
- Could be EXPERT WITHOUT A STAGE (10 years + published work + network)
- Could be SOLUTION SEEKER (technical capability, no deep domain credibility, willing to go narrow)

**Ruling: EXPERT WITHOUT A STAGE (override to Consulting-First SaaS)**
- Reasoning: The 10 years, published work, and 50+ network tips the balance to "expert in the eyes of the market." The published articles create credibility that can convert warm network into 2-3 consulting clients quickly. Once consulting delivers results, the founder becomes a recognized expert. This is faster to revenue and product validation than Hammering Deep, which requires 6+ months of authority building before product launch.

**If C chose Hammering Deep instead:**
- More conservative, less risky short-term (no expectation to close clients fast)
- But slower to revenue (6+ months building content + product before monetizing)
- Would be justified only if founder explicitly said "I want to avoid sales, prefer to build and market pure product"

### Borderline: LAUNCHER vs. OPERATOR

**Profile A (LAUNCHER):**
- $50K capital available
- Ex-corporate, leaving job to go full-time
- Strong decision-making ability
- Moderate execution track record (managed teams, launched features)
- Aggressive timeline (wants $100K MRR in 12 months)

**Profile B (OPERATOR):**
- $15K capital available (bootstrapped)
- Former COO, built systems and processes at 100-person company
- Detail-obsessed, wants to systematize every step
- Process-oriented track record (not execution-fast track record)
- Willing to move slower (2-3 year horizon to profitability)

**Archetype Decision:**

**Profile A = LAUNCHER** — Recommended Path: Replicate & Localize (or) Boring Micro-SaaS
- Capital justifies higher risk (Replicate means partnership/localization costs)
- Timeline aggression calls for capital deployment strategy
- Execution track record suggests they can orchestrate complex moves

**Profile B = OPERATOR** — Recommended Path: Replicate & Localize or Boring Micro-SaaS
- Systems expertise enables complex localization work (Replicate)
- Low capital suggests bootstrap micro-SaaS (simpler product, lower cost)
- Timeline allows for careful process design

**Borderline Case (Profile C):**
- $20K capital
- Ex-VP Ops (strong process background)
- Also shipped features directly (not just management)
- Medium timeline (18-month goal to $50K MRR)
- Currently undecided: "I can manage large projects OR I can bootstrap lean"

**Decision Tension:**
- Has Operator credibility (VP Ops, systems mindset)
- Has Launcher credibility (capital, shipped products, team background)
- Ambiguity on which strength to lead with

**Ruling: OPERATOR with LAUNCHER upside**
- Reasoning: Lead with Operator archetype (Replicate & Localize as primary path, leveraging systems expertise to operationalize partnerships + localization)
- But note: "You have enough capital to move aggressively. If you can find a co-founder who leads sales/growth, you could accelerate from Replicate to Boring Micro-SaaS + build portfolio of products. Your ops excellence scales to managing 2-3 products simultaneously better than 95% of founders."
- The OPERATOR path is safer and plays to primary strength; LAUNCHER upside is conditional on sales/distribution co-founder

### Borderline: SCRAMBLER vs. GHOST (Distinguishing the Unknowns)

**Profile A (SCRAMBLER):**
- First-time founder, age 28
- Variable skills: has coded, done sales consulting, built marketing campaigns
- Very limited capital (<$1K)
- Part-time commitment (20 hrs/week)
- High risk tolerance ("I'm experimenting to figure out what I want to build")
- ACTIVELY NETWORKING (joined 3 founder groups, goes to events)

**Profile B (GHOST):**
- First-time founder, age 28
- Variable skills: has coded, done sales consulting, built marketing campaigns
- Very limited capital (<$1K)
- Part-time commitment (20 hrs/week)
- High risk tolerance ("I'm experimenting")
- NO NETWORK OR VISIBILITY (not on social, not in groups, quiet builder)

**Archetype Decision:**

**Profile A = SCRAMBLER** — Recommended Path: Consulting-First SaaS
- High risk tolerance + active networking = can close early consulting clients
- Variable skills means: "Find the first customer, then let their problems clarify what to build"
- Networking creates distribution advantage even without brand

**Profile B = GHOST** — Recommended Path: Boring Micro-SaaS
- Same variable skills, but networking is zero
- Product-led growth is the only viable path (can't rely on personal brand or network)
- "Build something small, useful, boring; let the product do the talking"

**Key Distinguisher:** VISIBILITY + NETWORK (even without brand credentials) = SCRAMBLER. Complete absence of network/visibility = GHOST.

**Borderline Case (Profile C):**
- Variable skills
- <$1K capital
- 20 hrs/week
- Goes to 1-2 events per quarter (not actively networking, but not hermit either)
- Has 300 Twitter followers (posts occasionally about technical stuff, no personal brand)
- Actually published 2 open-source projects (has some visibility in developer community)

**Decision Tension:**
- More visible/networked than GHOST, but less active than SCRAMBLER
- Has some distributed audience (Twitter, open-source community)
- Clarity unclear on whether to lead with network or product

**Ruling: SCRAMBLER with GHOST fallback option**
- Reasoning: The open-source projects + 300 Twitter following + event attendance suggests option to go Consulting-First SaaS IF they package expertise well ("I built X for Y companies; can help others do the same").
- Alternative: If comfortable with "stealth builder" positioning, Boring Micro-SaaS also works — the open-source projects become proof of technical capability.
- Primary recommendation: Start SCRAMBLER path (package expertise from OSS projects as service), measure if 3 consulting clients possible in 30 days. If not, pivot to Boring Micro-SaaS (product speaks louder than founder).
