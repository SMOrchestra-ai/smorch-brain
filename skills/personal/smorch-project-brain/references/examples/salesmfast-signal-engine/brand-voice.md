# Brand Voice

## Voice Identity
- Brand personality: Technically sharp, commercially direct, regionally fluent. The voice of a senior revenue operator who has closed 200+ deals in the Gulf and now builds the infrastructure to systematize what used to require personal networks
- Tone spectrum: 70% authoritative / 20% provocative / 10% educational
- Voice anchor: "The signal-to-trust practitioner who builds in MENA, for MENA"

## Voice Attributes

### 1. Signal-Precise
Every claim references a specific signal, metric, or observation. No vague assertions.

- YES: "Your competitor posted 3 SDR job listings in the UAE last week. Your pipeline is about to get more competitive"
- NO: "Many companies are experiencing increased competition in the region"
- YES: "45% WhatsApp reply rate vs. 5% cold email in Gulf B2B"
- NO: "WhatsApp is an effective channel for the Middle East"

### 2. Commercially Direct
Lead with the revenue impact. Skip the methodology lecture.

- YES: "$48K/quarter in deals lost to late signal detection. One systematic fix prevents that"
- NO: "In the modern business environment, it's important to have a robust signal detection strategy"
- YES: "10 qualified meetings in 90 days or we work for free until you get them"
- NO: "We offer performance-based engagements with flexible outcomes"

### 3. Regionally Grounded
Default to MENA context. Gulf references, Arabic credibility signals, local market specifics.

- YES: "In Dubai, the deal was already in progress by the time your rep checked LinkedIn on Sunday morning"
- NO: "In today's global marketplace, timing is everything"
- YES: "WhatsApp voice note from a trusted referral > 50 cold emails"
- NO: "Leverage multiple communication channels for optimal engagement"

### 4. Anti-Generic
If a sentence could come from any SaaS company's website, kill it. Contrarian takes over consensus.

- YES: "Relationship-based selling is a tax on growth. Signal-based trust engineering is the replacement"
- NO: "We help you build better customer relationships"
- YES: "Your CRM is a system of record. You need a system of detection"
- NO: "Our platform integrates seamlessly with your existing tools"

## Words & Phrases

### Use These
| Word/Phrase | Context |
|------------|---------|
| Signal detection | Core product function |
| Signal-triggered outreach | How outreach works (not "automated outreach") |
| Buying intent | What we detect |
| Pipeline infrastructure | What we build (not "pipeline generation") |
| Trust engineering | Our methodology (not "relationship building") |
| Revenue operations | Our domain (not "sales enablement") |
| Proof of competence | What we deliver to prospects (not "value proposition") |
| Detect, score, sequence | Our 3-step process |
| System of detection | What Signal Engine is (contrasted with CRM as "system of record") |
| Gulf enterprise | Our target market |

### Avoid These
| Word/Phrase | Why | Replace With |
|------------|-----|--------------|
| AI-powered | Every tool claims AI. Table stakes, not differentiation | "Signal detection from 10 MENA data sources" |
| End-to-end | Means nothing specific | Name the specific workflow: "detect → score → sequence → deploy" |
| Cutting-edge / innovative | Self-awarded superlatives | Specific proof: "First platform to index DIFC registration signals" |
| Digital transformation | Banned. Means nothing | "Automated signal detection replacing manual LinkedIn scanning" |
| Best-in-class | Unverifiable | Specific comparison: "4x the MENA signal coverage of ZoomInfo" |
| Seamlessly integrates | Every vendor says this | "Feeds signals directly into your existing CRM pipeline view" |
| Leverage | Banned per global instructions | "Use," "deploy," "apply" |
| Solution / platform (standalone) | Generic | "Signal Engine," "outbound intelligence system" |

## Content Guidelines by Channel

### LinkedIn Posts
- Length: 150-300 words. One core idea per post
- Structure: Pattern interrupt opening (1-2 lines) → Insight/framework → Proof point → Soft CTA
- Language: English primary (LinkedIn audience is international). Arabic opening line for MENA-targeted posts boosts engagement
- Tone: 30% teaching, 70% opinion. Take a position. "Signal detection replaces relationship-dependent selling" not "signal detection can complement your existing approach"
- Hashtags: 3-5 max. #B2BSales #SignalBasedGTM #MENATech #OutboundSales

### Cold Email
- Length: 3-5 sentences. First sentence: signal-based observation about the prospect. Last sentence: low-friction CTA
- Tone: Direct, specific, zero fluff. Sound like a peer who noticed something relevant, not a vendor pitching
- Subject line: Reference the signal, not the product. "Re: your 3 new SDR hires in Dubai" not "Introducing SalesMfast Signal Engine"
- Signature: Name + title + one proof point ("Signal-based outbound for 200+ Gulf B2B teams")

### WhatsApp (Warm Leads)
- Length: 2-3 short messages, not one wall of text
- Tone: Conversational Gulf Arabic for MENA leads, English for international. Open with greeting ("السلام عليكم" or referencing how you connected)
- Structure: Message 1: greeting + context ("We connected at GITEX"). Message 2: value hook ("Noticed your team is expanding SDR coverage"). Message 3: CTA ("Worth a 15-min call this week?")
- Voice notes: Acceptable and culturally preferred for follow-ups. Keep under 60 seconds

### Proposals / Decks
- Tone: Authoritative, data-rich, commercially grounded
- Structure: Problem with cost quantification → Unique mechanism → Proof points → Offer with ROI → Risk reversal
- Visuals: Dark theme preferred. Data visualizations over stock photos. Signal flow diagrams over feature screenshots
- Language: English for proposals. Arabic executive summary if buyer is Arabic-primary

## Formatting Rules (Project-Specific)
- Signal references: Always bold the signal when referencing it in outreach. "**Your competitor hired 3 SDRs** last week" not "Your competitor hired 3 SDRs last week"
- Numbers: Specific over round. "$48K/quarter" not "nearly $50K." "3.2 weeks" not "about a month"
- CTAs: Always low-friction. "15-minute signal review" or "quick pipeline diagnostic" — never "schedule a demo" or "let's discuss your needs"
- Competitor mentions: By name, with specific differentiator. "Unlike 6sense, we index MENA signals" not "Unlike competitors, we're different"
