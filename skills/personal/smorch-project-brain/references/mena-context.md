This is the DEFAULT reference for all projects (not conditional). Even non-MENA projects should check this file to explicitly note "Non-MENA project: skip regional adaptation." MENA is Mamoun's primary market. When in doubt, default to MENA context.

## TRUST MECHANICS BY COUNTRY

### UAE (Dubai, Abu Dhabi)
- **Trust threshold:** HIGH. Prospects need 3-5 touchpoints before considering a call. Social proof from known names (DIFC companies, government entities, known brands) accelerates trust
- **Language:** English is business default in Dubai. Arabic builds deeper credibility. Bilingual (Arabic headline, English body) outperforms monolingual
- **Channel priority:** LinkedIn (cold) → WhatsApp (warm) → Email (nurture). WhatsApp Business reply rate: 45-60% vs email 5-8%
- **Meeting culture:** In-person meetings still close deals. "Coffee meeting" is a real sales step, not metaphorical. But systematic signal detection reduces the number needed from 10 to 2-3
- **Payment:** Credit card adoption high. Stripe works. Local preference: Tabby (BNPL), Tap Payments. Wire transfer for enterprise (>$10K)
- **Decision speed:** Faster than Saudi/Kuwait. 2-4 week sales cycle for SME, 2-3 months for enterprise
- **Key signals:** New DIFC/ADGM license registrations, job posts on Bayt.com and LinkedIn, company relocations to Dubai

### Saudi Arabia (Riyadh, Jeddah)
- **Trust threshold:** VERY HIGH. Family/tribal network endorsements carry weight. Cold outreach has lowest response rate in Gulf without warm introduction
- **Language:** Arabic is MANDATORY for credibility. English-only outreach signals "outsider." Formal Arabic (close to MSA but not stiff) for initial contact, shift to casual Saudi dialect once relationship established
- **Channel priority:** WhatsApp (primary for everything) → LinkedIn (discovery) → Email (formal follow-up only). WhatsApp group endorsements are powerful
- **Meeting culture:** Vision 2030 is accelerating digital adoption but face-to-face still dominates. Riyadh moves faster than Jeddah on tech adoption
- **Payment:** STC Pay growing fast. Mada (local debit network). HyperPay for payment processing. Wire transfer standard for B2B
- **Decision speed:** Slower. 3-6 month enterprise sales cycle. Multiple stakeholders, often requires executive sponsor. Ramadan and Hajj seasons slow everything
- **Key signals:** GOSI registration data, Tadawul announcements, Vision 2030 contract awards, Ministry of Investment activity

### Egypt (Cairo)
- **Trust threshold:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Price sensitivity is higher than Gulf. Proof of ROI is essential: "how much will I save" beats "how much better will I be"
- **Language:** Egyptian Arabic (not Gulf, not MSA). English acceptable for tech audiences but Arabic builds trust with SMEs
- **Channel priority:** WhatsApp (dominant) → Facebook/Instagram (discovery for SME) → LinkedIn (enterprise) → Email (nurture)
- **Payment:** Fawry (cash payment network), Paymob, local bank transfers. Credit card penetration lower than Gulf. Installment plans increase conversion 2-3x
- **Decision speed:** Fast for SME (<$500/month). Slow for enterprise (government/institutional buyers: 6-12 months)
- **Key signals:** Startup Egypt ecosystem activity, ITIDA announcements, Cairo tech hub expansion

### Jordan (Amman)
- **Trust threshold:** HIGH. Small market where everyone knows everyone. One bad reference kills a deal. Community endorsement is make-or-break
- **Language:** Levantine Arabic. English common in tech circles. Bilingual content works well
- **Channel priority:** WhatsApp → LinkedIn → Email. Small enough market that personal introductions outperform cold outreach
- **Payment:** eFawateercom, Zain Cash, bank transfers. Limited credit card adoption outside Amman
- **Decision speed:** Fast when trust is established. Small community means referrals close faster

### Qatar / Kuwait
- **Trust threshold:** VERY HIGH. Smallest markets in Gulf. Government and semi-government buyers dominate. Requires local partner or established brand presence
- **Language:** Arabic mandatory. English supplementary
- **Channel priority:** Personal network → WhatsApp → LinkedIn. Cold outreach extremely low success rate without introduction
- **Payment:** QPay (Qatar), KNET (Kuwait). Enterprise: wire transfer through local bank
- **Decision speed:** Slow. Government procurement cycles: 6-12 months. Private sector faster but still relationship-dependent

## CHANNEL RULES FOR MENA

### WhatsApp
- **Status:** Primary warm channel for ALL MENA markets
- **Tone:** Conversational, not formal. Greetings matter ("السلام عليكم" before business). Voice notes are culturally normal and build trust faster than text
- **Timing:** Sunday-Thursday. Avoid Friday (prayer day). Best response times: 10am-12pm, 4pm-6pm local time
- **Etiquette:** Don't pitch in first message. Start with introduction + context ("We met at GITEX" or "Ahmed referred me"). Second message: value delivery. Third message: soft ask
- **Group dynamics:** WhatsApp groups are where trust signals live. Being endorsed in a relevant group is the highest-trust signal in MENA

### LinkedIn
- **Status:** Primary cold/discovery channel. Growing fast in MENA, especially UAE and Saudi
- **Tone:** Professional but not stiff. Arabic posts get higher engagement from MENA audience. English posts reach broader audience
- **Connection requests:** Always include a note. Reference a shared connection, event, or the prospect's recent post. Never send blank connection requests
- **Content:** Contrarian takes on MENA tech, Arabic-English mixed content, case studies from named Gulf companies outperform generic content 5-10x

### Email
- **Status:** Secondary/nurture channel. Lower open rates in MENA than US/EU (15-20% vs 25-35%)
- **When to use:** Follow-up after warm engagement (LinkedIn reply, WhatsApp conversation, event meeting). NOT for cold first touch in Gulf markets
- **Format:** Short. 3-5 sentences max. Arabic subject line + English body is a tested pattern for bilingual audiences
- **Infrastructure:** Avoid .com domains that look American. .ae or .sa domains build local credibility

### Events / In-Person
- **Status:** High-trust but high-cost channel. GITEX, LEAP, regional tech events are pipeline generators
- **Signal value:** Attending the same event is a trust signal. "I saw you at GITEX" is a legitimate opener
- **Follow-up:** WhatsApp within 24 hours of meeting. NOT email

## ARABIC CONTENT RULES

### Dialect Selection
| Market | Arabic Style | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| UAE, Saudi, Qatar, Kuwait | Gulf conversational | Not MSA formal. Mix English tech terms naturally ("AI automation" not "أتمتة الذكاء الاصطناعي") |
| Egypt | Egyptian colloquial | Different vocabulary and grammar. Do not use Gulf expressions |
| Jordan, Lebanon, Palestine | Levantine | Closest to MSA but still casual. Don't use Gulf slang |
| Pan-Arab | Light MSA | Only when targeting all Arabic speakers simultaneously. Feels formal but universally understood |

### Bilingual Rules
- **Headlines:** Arabic for MENA audience, English for global/mixed
- **Body text:** Follow the audience's primary language. If ICP is a Gulf-based CTO who reads LinkedIn in English, write English body with Arabic greeting
- **Technical terms:** Keep in English. Translating "CRM" or "API" to Arabic sounds unnatural and reduces credibility
- **Brand names:** Never translate. "SalesMfast" stays as-is, never "سيلز إم فاست"
- **RTL layout:** When producing Arabic-primary content, ensure right-to-left layout. Headers right-aligned. Numbers stay LTR within RTL text

### Tone Calibration
- **Avoid:** MSA formal that sounds like a government document. Literal translations from English that lose cultural nuance
- **Target:** The tone of a smart, successful Gulf business person explaining something to a peer over coffee
- **Test:** Read it aloud. If it sounds like a textbook, rewrite. If it sounds like a conversation, ship it
