# Positioning Framework — Synthesized from Dunford + Hormozi + Brunson

## PURPOSE
Use this when generating positioning.md. Ensures positioning is sharp, defensible, and commercially grounded.

---

## THE POSITIONING STACK (5 layers, build in order)

### Layer 1: Market Category
What shelf does this sit on in the buyer's mind?

**Test:** If the buyer had to explain what this is to their boss in 5 words, what would they say?
- Good: "Signal-based outbound platform"
- Bad: "AI-powered comprehensive business solution"

**Rules:**
- Pick a category the buyer already understands. Don't invent categories unless you have $10M+ to educate the market
- If choosing between two categories, pick the one where you'd be the obvious winner in the subset
- Category should be searchable (the buyer would Google this term)

### Layer 2: Competitive Alternatives (April Dunford)
What would the customer do if your product/service didn't exist?

**Key insight:** Alternatives are NOT just direct competitors. They include:
- Doing nothing (status quo)
- Hiring someone (consultant, employee)
- Building it themselves (spreadsheets, internal tools)
- Using an adjacent tool (CRM for outbound, when it's really a spreadsheet problem)

**For each alternative, document:**
- What it is
- Why customers use it today
- Its critical weakness (what it can't do that you can)
- Switching cost (what it takes to move away from it)

### Layer 3: Unique Mechanism (Alex Hormozi)
What is the specific HOW that makes your solution work when alternatives don't?

**The Hormozi Test:** "Unlike [alternatives], we [unique mechanism] which means [specific outcome]"

**Rules:**
- Must be STRUCTURAL, not just "better." "Better UX" is not a mechanism. "Systematic signal detection from 10 real-time data sources" is a mechanism
- Must be defensible: something competitors can't easily copy
- Must be explainable in one sentence
- The mechanism is the WEDGE: the sharp point of the spear

**Mechanism types (strongest → weakest):**
1. Proprietary data/process (strongest: "We index 10M MENA business signals daily")
2. Unique methodology ("Signal-to-Trust framework based on 200+ Gulf enterprise deals")
3. Network effects ("Every user improves the signal library for all users")
4. Integration depth ("Only platform that connects Clay + GHL + Instantly into one workflow")
5. Speed advantage ("Detect and sequence in <48 hours vs. industry average of 2 weeks")
6. "Better/faster/cheaper" (weakest: easily copied, doesn't create loyalty)

### Layer 4: Positioning Statement
Combine layers 1-3 into a single statement.

**Template:**
For [ICP: specific role at specific company type] who [situation: what they struggle with], [product/service name] is the only [category] that [unique mechanism] so they can [dream outcome] without [biggest objection/risk].

**Rules:**
- One sentence. If it needs two, the positioning isn't sharp enough
- "The only" is intentional: if you can't say "the only," your mechanism isn't unique enough
- Dream outcome must be quantified where possible
- "Without [objection]" addresses the #1 reason buyers hesitate

### Layer 5: One-Sentence Wedge
The pattern-interrupt opener that goes into outbound messaging.

**Template (Signal-to-Trust):**
"[Observed signal] is costing you [specific outcome]. Here's the [one-line solution]."

**Rules:**
- Must reference something the prospect ACTUALLY did (a signal, not an assumption)
- Must name a specific cost (dollars, deals, hours — not vague "challenges")
- Must imply a solution without spelling it out (create curiosity, not a pitch)
- Must pass the "would I stop scrolling?" test

**Examples:**
- Strong: "Your competitor just posted 3 SDR job listings. Your pipeline is about to get more competitive. One systematic change prevents that."
- Weak: "Many companies struggle with outbound. Our AI-powered platform helps."

## POSITIONING ANTI-PATTERNS (Reject These)

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Fix |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| "We're the Uber of X" | Lazy analogy, doesn't explain mechanism | Name the specific mechanism |
| "AI-powered" as differentiator | Every tool claims AI. It's table stakes, not positioning | Name what the AI specifically does that competitors' AI doesn't |
| "End-to-end solution" | Means nothing specific. Could describe any tool | Name the 1-2 things you do that no one else does |
| "For businesses of all sizes" | No ICP clarity. You serve no one when you serve everyone | Name the specific company size, role, and industry |
| "Easy to use" | Subjective, unverifiable, not a mechanism | Name the specific workflow reduction (e.g., "3 clicks to launch a campaign vs. 47 in competing tools") |
| "Best-in-class" / "World-class" | Self-awarded superlatives. Zero credibility | Replace with specific proof: "Used by 200+ MENA enterprises" |
| "Digital transformation" | Banned term. Means nothing. | Name the specific outcome: "automated signal detection" |
