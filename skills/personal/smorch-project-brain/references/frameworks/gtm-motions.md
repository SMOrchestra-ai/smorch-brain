# GTM Motions Framework — 13 Motions with Scoring Criteria

## PURPOSE
Use this when generating gtm-channels.md. Ranks applicable motions based on ICP access, founder capability, and MENA market fit.

---

## THE 13 GTM MOTIONS

### 1. Dream 100
**What:** Identify 100 highest-value target accounts. Build signal monitoring. Sequence proof of competence at each account.
**Best for:** B2B with identifiable named accounts. Consulting, agency, enterprise tech.
**MENA fit:** HIGH — relationship-driven markets respond well to targeted, personalized approaches.
**Effort:** Medium setup, low ongoing. Requires good targeting data.
**Timeline:** First meetings within 2-4 weeks.

### 2. Authority Education
**What:** Publish educational content that demonstrates expertise. Training, frameworks, case studies. Builds trust before the sales conversation.
**Best for:** Consulting, training, any offer where credibility drives purchase. Mamoun's YouTube channel is an example.
**MENA fit:** HIGH — Gulf market values demonstrated expertise over feature claims. Training builds authority, authority builds trust, trust sells.
**Effort:** High content creation upfront. Medium ongoing.
**Timeline:** Authority compounds. 4-8 weeks before pipeline impact.

### 3. Outcome Demo
**What:** Show real customers getting real results. Live demos, case studies with numbers, before/after screenshots.
**Best for:** Tech products, agency work where results are visual/quantifiable.
**MENA fit:** HIGH — "show me it works for someone like me" is the trust accelerator in MENA.
**Effort:** Low if you have case studies. High if you need to create them.
**Timeline:** Immediate impact when demos exist.

### 4. Signal Sniper
**What:** Detect high-intent signals (job posts, tech stack changes, funding events, competitor activity) and trigger personalized outreach within 48 hours.
**Best for:** B2B outbound. Any ICP where buying triggers are detectable.
**MENA fit:** MEDIUM — fewer public signal sources in MENA vs. US. But signals that exist (GOSI data, DIFC registrations, LinkedIn activity) are higher-signal because fewer competitors are tracking them.
**Effort:** High setup (data infrastructure). Low ongoing once built.
**Timeline:** First signals within days. Pipeline in 2-4 weeks.

### 5. Value Trust (Social Proof)
**What:** Third-party validation: testimonials, partner logos, media mentions, certifications. Let others say you're good.
**Best for:** Any offer where trust is the primary barrier.
**MENA fit:** VERY HIGH — social proof from known Gulf brands is the #1 trust accelerator. One logo from a known entity > 50 feature slides.
**Effort:** Low effort to collect, medium effort to deploy systematically.
**Timeline:** Immediate once assets exist.

### 6. Waitlist Heat
**What:** Build pre-launch demand with a waitlist. Create scarcity and FOMO before the product/service is available.
**Best for:** New product launches, cohort training, community.
**MENA fit:** MEDIUM — works for tech-savvy audiences. Less effective with traditional B2B buyers.
**Effort:** Low. Landing page + email sequence.
**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks to build meaningful list.

### 7. 7x4x11 (Account-Based)
**What:** 7 personalized messages, 4-day spacing, 11 target accounts per campaign. Intense, personalized account-based outreach.
**Best for:** Enterprise deals, high-ACV products/services.
**MENA fit:** MEDIUM — works but must be adapted. WhatsApp counts as a touch. In-person meeting counts as a touch.
**Effort:** High per-account. Only works at small scale (11 accounts at a time).
**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks per cycle.

### 8. BOFU SEO
**What:** Target bottom-of-funnel keywords: "best [category] for [use case]", "[competitor] alternatives", "how to [solve problem]".
**Best for:** Tech products with self-serve onboarding.
**MENA fit:** LOW-MEDIUM — Arabic SEO is underserved (opportunity) but search volume is lower. English BOFU works for global audience.
**Effort:** High upfront (content creation). Low ongoing.
**Timeline:** 3-6 months for organic results.

### 9. Feature Launches
**What:** Treat every feature release as a mini-campaign. Announcements, demos, user stories around new capabilities.
**Best for:** Established tech products with active user base.
**MENA fit:** MEDIUM — works as part of broader motion, not standalone.
**Effort:** Low per launch.
**Timeline:** Ongoing, event-driven.

### 10. Build-in-Public
**What:** Share the journey transparently. Revenue numbers, challenges, decisions, learnings. Builds audience through authenticity.
**Best for:** Solo founders, early-stage products, personal brands.
**MENA fit:** LOW-MEDIUM — Gulf culture is more private about business details. Works better for diaspora founders or tech-savvy audiences on Twitter/LinkedIn.
**Effort:** Low. Consistent posting.
**Timeline:** 2-3 months before meaningful audience.

### 11. Wave Riding
**What:** Attach your messaging to trending events, news, or cultural moments. Surf existing attention waves.
**Best for:** Content creators, brands with fast execution capability.
**MENA fit:** MEDIUM — Ramadan, National Days, GITEX, LEAP are predictable waves. Industry news (Vision 2030 announcements) are high-value.
**Effort:** Low per wave but requires fast execution.
**Timeline:** Immediate but unpredictable.

### 12. Paid VSL
**What:** Video Sales Letter driven by paid traffic. Facebook/LinkedIn/YouTube ads → VSL → application/purchase.
**Best for:** Scalable offers with proven messaging. Training, mid-ticket consulting.
**MENA fit:** LOW-MEDIUM — CPM in MENA is lower than US (opportunity) but conversion rates vary. Arabic VSL for MENA, English for global.
**Effort:** High upfront (VSL production + ad creative). Medium ongoing (ad management).
**Timeline:** Days to launch, weeks to optimize.

### 13. LTD (Lifetime Deal)
**What:** Offer lifetime access at a one-time price. Used for initial traction and user base building.
**Best for:** SaaS products needing early users/feedback. AppSumo, PitchGround model.
**MENA fit:** LOW — Gulf B2B buyers are suspicious of "too good to be true" deals. Works better for global audience.
**Effort:** Low setup. High support burden.
**Timeline:** Immediate sales, but revenue is capped.

## MOTION SCORING CRITERIA

Score each motion 1-10 on 4 dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | What to Evaluate |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| **ICP Fit** | 30% | Does this motion reach the specific ICP? Are they on this channel? Would they engage with this format? |
| **Founder Capability** | 25% | Can Mamoun (or the client's team) actually execute this? Do they have the skills, time, and tools? |
| **MENA Market Fit** | 25% | Does this motion work in the target MENA market? Trust mechanics, language, channel preferences? |
| **Speed to Pipeline** | 20% | How quickly does this generate qualified conversations? Days, weeks, or months? |

**Composite score:** (ICP Fit × 0.30) + (Founder Capability × 0.25) + (MENA Fit × 0.25) + (Speed × 0.20)

### Tier Assignment
| Composite Score | Tier | Action |
|----------------|------|--------|
| 8.0+ | PRIMARY | Execute immediately. Top 3 motions |
| 6.0-7.9 | SECONDARY | Activate in month 2-3 |
| 4.0-5.9 | CONDITIONAL | Only if primary motions saturate |
| <4.0 | SKIP | Don't invest time here |

## CHANNEL SELECTION RULES

### For MENA B2B Projects
- LinkedIn (cold discovery) + WhatsApp (warm conversion) is the default stack
- Email is secondary/nurture, not primary outreach
- Events are high-value for enterprise targets (GITEX, LEAP, sector-specific)
- Arabic content on LinkedIn outperforms English for MENA-focused targeting

### For US/EU B2B Projects
- Email (cold) + LinkedIn (warm) is the default stack
- WhatsApp is not a business channel in US/EU
- Content marketing (BOFU SEO, Authority Education) has higher leverage

### For Training / Community Projects
- YouTube (content discovery) + WhatsApp groups (community) + Email (nurture)
- LinkedIn for professional audience, Instagram/TikTok for SME/consumer audience
- Webinar launch is the standard GTM for training products
