# Build-in-Public Trust Flywheel — Motion Assets

**Best for:** Founders who can share progress transparently to build audience and trust simultaneously.
**MENA Note:** Rare in MENA — founders who do this stand out immediately. Lower competition = higher impact.

---

## Assets Produced

### 1. Build-in-Public Content Calendar (DOCX)
**File:** `build-in-public/bip-content-calendar.docx`

30-day build-in-public content plan.

```
CONTENT PILLARS (rotate):
A. NUMBERS: Revenue, users, metrics (transparent, specific)
B. LESSONS: What you learned this week (wins AND failures)
C. PROCESS: How you built something (screenshots, decisions)
D. ASKS: Questions to your audience (input, feedback, votes)

WEEK 1:
Day 1: "I'm building [X] in public. Here's why and what to expect." (A)
Day 3: "First decision: [tech/business choice]. Here's my reasoning." (C)
Day 5: "Day 5 update: [metric]. Here's what's working and what's not." (A+B)
Day 7: "Question for [ICP]: would you use [feature]? Why/why not?" (D)

WEEK 2-4: Rotate through pillars, increasing specificity.
Each post references real numbers, real decisions, real challenges.
```


### 3. Build-in-Public Platform Strategy (DOCX)
**File:** `build-in-public/platform-strategy.docx`

```
WHERE TO BUILD IN PUBLIC:

PRIMARY (daily/weekly):
- LinkedIn: B2B audience, professional credibility
- Twitter/X: Indie hacker / startup community

SECONDARY (weekly):
- YouTube: Monthly recap videos, longer builds
- Newsletter: Deep-dive updates for subscribers
- Indie Hackers / relevant community

MENA-SPECIFIC:
- WhatsApp status: Quick visual updates
- Arabic tech communities on Telegram
- Arabic Twitter (different audience than English)

RULES:
1. Be specific with numbers — "$1,247 MRR" not "growing"
2. Share failures as openly as wins
3. Respond to every comment (community building)
4. Tag people who helped or inspired you
5. Never make it sound perfect — authenticity is the point
```

---

## MENA Adaptations

- Almost NO one builds in public in Arabic. This is a massive differentiation opportunity.
- Gulf audiences respect transparency but it's culturally unusual in business — which makes it stand out more.
- Revenue sharing is sensitive — consider sharing growth % instead of absolute numbers if preferred.
