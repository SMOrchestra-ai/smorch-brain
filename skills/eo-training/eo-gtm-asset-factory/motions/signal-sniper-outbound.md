# Signal Sniper Outbound — Motion Assets

**Best for:** B2B founders with clear ICP who want to find and reach buyers showing intent signals.
**MENA Note:** Cold outbound is harder in MENA (lower email open rates, higher trust threshold). Signal-based outbound solves this by only reaching out when intent is detected.

---

## Assets Produced

### 1. Cold Email 3-Step Sequence (DOCX)
**File:** `signal-sniper-outbound/cold-email-3step.docx`

3-email cold outreach sequence. Short, sharp, signal-referenced.

```
EMAIL 1: THE SIGNAL REFERENCE
Subject options (A/B):
A: "[Signal they triggered] — quick question"
B: "Noticed [specific thing about their company]"

Body:
"[First Name],

Saw that [specific signal: job posting, funding round, tech change, expansion].

When companies [do that signal], they usually run into [specific pain from ICP].

[Venture Name] helps [ICP] [specific outcome] — [one proof point].

Worth 15 min? [Calendly link]

[Signature]"

Rules: Under 80 words. Reference a REAL signal. No "I hope this finds you well."
```

```
EMAIL 2: THE VALUE ADD (Day 3)
Subject: "Re: [previous subject]"

Body:
"[First Name],

Quick follow-up. Put together a [resource/insight/analysis] that might help
with [the challenge from email 1].

[Link or 2-sentence summary of the value piece]

Happy to walk through it. [Calendly link]

[Signature]"

Rules: Under 60 words. Add value, don't just follow up.
```

```
EMAIL 3: THE BREAKUP (Day 7)
Subject: "Closing the loop"

Body:
"[First Name],

Looks like timing's off. Totally get it.

If [pain/signal context] becomes a priority, here's where to find me: [link]

All the best with [their initiative from signal].

[Signature]"

Rules: Under 40 words. No guilt. No "just checking in." Clean exit.
```

### 2. LinkedIn Connection Sequence (DOCX)
**File:** `signal-sniper-outbound/linkedin-sequence.docx`

```
CONNECTION REQUEST:
"[First Name] — [mutual connection/group/event reference].
Building in [their space]. Would love to connect."
(Under 50 chars if possible — short requests get higher acceptance)

MESSAGE 1 (after acceptance, Day 1):
"Thanks for connecting, [First Name].

Saw your work on [specific thing]. Relevant to what we're building at [Venture Name] —
[one sentence what you do].

No pitch — just connecting with people in the space."

MESSAGE 2 (Day 5):
"[First Name] — been thinking about [topic related to their work].

[One insight or question that shows you've done homework]

Curious how you're handling [specific challenge]?"

MESSAGE 3 (Day 10):
"[First Name] — we help [ICP] [outcome].

Just produced [result] for [similar company/persona].

Worth a quick chat? Happy to share how. [Calendly]"
```

### 3. Signal Scoring Criteria (DOCX)
**File:** `signal-sniper-outbound/signal-scoring-criteria.docx`

```
INTENT SIGNALS TO MONITOR:

TIER 1 — HIGH INTENT (reach out within 48 hours):
□ Job posting for role your product replaces/supports
□ Funding round announced (new budget available)
□ Tech stack change detected (new tool adoption)
□ Competitor mentioned in their content (looking for alternatives)
□ Direct search for your category keywords

TIER 2 — MEDIUM INTENT (reach out within 1 week):
□ Company expansion to new market (especially MENA)
□ New C-level hire (new priorities coming)
□ Industry event attendance (in-market, learning)
□ Content engagement on relevant topics
□ Team growth signals (hiring spree = growing pains)

TIER 3 — LOW INTENT (nurture, don't pitch):
□ Profile views on your company page
□ Likes/comments on industry content
□ General category interest without specific action
□ Follows your company or founder

SIGNAL SOURCES:
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator (job changes, company updates)
- Google Alerts (company mentions, funding news)
- BuiltWith / Wappalyzer (tech stack changes)
- Clay.com (enrichment + signal detection automation)
- Crunchbase (funding rounds)
- Twitter/X (public conversations)

DISQUALIFICATION SIGNALS (do NOT reach out):
✗ Company size below [minimum from icp.md]
✗ No budget authority (wrong title/level)
✗ Already using a direct competitor (and happy)
✗ Wrong geography (outside target markets)
✗ Recent layoffs (budget frozen)
```

### 4. WhatsApp Cold-Warm Bridge (DOCX)
**File:** `signal-sniper-outbound/whatsapp-bridge.docx`

For MENA markets where WhatsApp is the preferred business channel.

```
MESSAGE 1 (after LinkedIn connection or email reply):
"Hey [Name], [Your name] here from [Venture Name].

Thanks for connecting on [LinkedIn/email].

Easier to chat here? Quick question about [their signal/challenge].

[Voice note option: 20-second intro]"

MESSAGE 2 (if positive response):
"Perfect. Here's a quick look at what we do:
[30-second screen recording or screenshot]

If it's relevant, happy to show you more.
When works for a quick call?"

MESSAGE 3 (booking follow-up):
"Great — locked in for [date/time].

I'll prepare [something specific for them] beforehand.

Talk soon 👋"
```

---

## MENA Adaptations

- Cold email open rates in MENA are 15-25% lower than US/EU. Signal-based targeting is essential.
- WhatsApp bridge after email/LinkedIn dramatically increases response rates in Gulf markets.
- Arabic subject lines in cold email get 20-30% higher open rates for Arabic-speaking ICPs.
- Always reference a mutual connection if one exists — cold without context is much harder in MENA.
- Voice notes on WhatsApp outperform text messages for first business contact in Saudi/UAE.
