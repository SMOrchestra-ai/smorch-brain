# Authority Education Engine — Motion Assets

**Best for:** Founders selling into markets where buyers need to believe you understand the problem better than anyone.
**MENA Note:** Higher context, hierarchical markets. Authority content builds trust faster than feature lists.

---

## Assets Produced

### 1. YouTube Script Templates (DOCX)
**File:** `authority-education/youtube-scripts.docx`

3 script formats, each tailored to the founder's niche and ICP pains.

**Format A: Tutorial Script (8-12 min)**
```
HOOK (0:00-0:30):
"[Specific outcome] in [timeframe]. Here's exactly how."
[Show the end result first — outcome demo in 15 seconds]

INTRO (0:30-1:00):
"I'm [Name]. I [credibility in one sentence from founderprofile.md].
Today I'm going to show you [specific process] that [outcome]."

CONTENT (1:00-9:00):
Step 1: [Action] — [Why this step matters for ICP]
Step 2: [Action] — [Common mistake to avoid]
Step 3: [Action] — [Pro tip from founder's experience]
[Each step: show, don't tell. Screen share or visual]

CTA (9:00-10:00):
"If you want [bigger outcome], [Venture Name] does this automatically.
Link in the description. [Low-friction CTA]."

OUTRO (10:00):
"Drop a comment if you tried this. Subscribe for more [topic]."
```

**Format B: Contrarian Take Script (5-8 min)**
```
HOOK: "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's why."
THESIS: State the contrarian position with one proof point.
EVIDENCE: 3 specific examples or data points.
REFRAME: "Instead of [old way], do [new way]."
CTA: "I built [Venture Name] on this exact principle. [Link]."
```

**Format C: Case Study Script (10-15 min)**
```
HOOK: "[Client/user] went from [before state] to [after state] in [time]."
CONTEXT: Who they are, what they were struggling with.
PROCESS: What we did, step by step.
RESULTS: Specific numbers and outcomes.
LESSONS: 3 takeaways the viewer can apply.
CTA: "Want similar results? [Venture Name] — link below."
```

### 3. Webinar Structure (DOCX)
**File:** `authority-education/webinar-structure.docx`

60-minute webinar flow adapted from Perfect Webinar framework.

```
MINUTE 0-5: WELCOME + PROMISE
"By the end of this session, you'll have [specific outcome]."
Set expectations. No fluff intro.

MINUTE 5-15: ORIGIN STORY
From founderprofile.md — how you discovered the problem.
Key moments: the frustration, the insight, the build.
Connect your story to THEIR pain.

MINUTE 15-25: SECRET #1
[Reframe from positioning.md wedge angle 1]
"Most people think [common belief]. The reality is [contrarian insight]."
Support with data or example.

MINUTE 25-35: SECRET #2
[From positioning.md wedge angle 2]
"Here's what nobody tells you about [topic]."
Show proof (demo, screenshot, case study).

MINUTE 35-45: SECRET #3
[From unique mechanism]
"This is the specific approach that makes [outcome] possible."
Connect all three secrets together.

MINUTE 45-55: THE OFFER
Stack the value (not a hard sell, a logical next step):
"Here's everything you get with [Venture Name]..."
Price reveal. Bonuses if applicable. Urgency element.

MINUTE 55-60: Q&A + CLOSE
Answer 3-5 questions. Each answer reinforces a selling point.
Final CTA with deadline.
```

|-----|----------|-------------|-------|------|-----|--------|

**Content mix (30 days):**
- 4 YouTube videos (1/week): 2 tutorials, 1 contrarian, 1 case study
- 12 LinkedIn posts (3/week): mix of insights, stories, value posts
- 8 LinkedIn comments on Dream 100 posts (2/week)

**Topic generation rules:**
- Every topic maps to an ICP pain from icp.md
- Every topic connects to the founder's positioning
- Alternate between teaching, storytelling, and proof
- Never repeat the same angle in the same week

## MENA Adaptations

- YouTube: Arabic content performs significantly better for MENA audience. Consider Arabic titles even if video is English.
- LinkedIn: English is standard for B2B MENA LinkedIn. But Arabic posts get higher engagement from local audience.
- Webinar: Gulf audiences expect formal but warm tone. Start with brief personal connection. Ramadan-aware scheduling.
- Lead magnets: PDF works universally. Email courses may underperform — WhatsApp delivery performs better in MENA.
