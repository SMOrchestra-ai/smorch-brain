# Waitlist Heat-to-Webinar Close — Motion Assets

**Best for:** When the offer needs explanation and trust, and you want a controlled launch cycle.
**MENA Note:** One of the strongest motions for MENA. Matches how trust is built here — personal, educational, community-driven.

---

## Assets Produced

### 1. Waitlist Landing Page Copy (DOCX)
**File:** `waitlist-webinar/waitlist-page-copy.docx`

Copy blocks for a waitlist page (can be added to the core landing page or separate).

```
HEADLINE: "[Outcome] is coming to [market/niche]. Get early access."

SUBHEAD: "[Venture Name] helps [ICP] do [outcome] without [pain].
Join [X] founders already on the waitlist."

WHAT YOU GET:
✓ Early access before public launch
✓ [Founding member pricing / bonus]
✓ [Exclusive resource or community access]
✓ [Direct line to the founder for feedback]

FORM: Name + Email + [Optional: WhatsApp number]
BUTTON: "Join the Waitlist" or "Get Early Access"

TRUST ELEMENTS:
- "[X] founders already joined"
- "Launching [month/timeframe]"
- "No credit card required"
```

### 2. Waitlist Nurture Email Sequence (DOCX)
**File:** `waitlist-webinar/waitlist-nurture-sequence.docx`

5-email sequence for nurturing waitlist subscribers toward webinar + purchase.

```
EMAIL 1: WELCOME (immediately after signup)
Subject: "You're in — here's what happens next"
Body: Thank them. Tell them what to expect. Give one immediate value piece.
CTA: "Reply and tell me: what's your biggest challenge with [topic]?"

EMAIL 2: VALUE (Day 3)
Subject: "The [X] mistake [ICP] makes with [topic]"
Body: Teach one useful thing. Reference their pain. Show you understand.
CTA: "More on this in our upcoming live session — save your spot: [webinar link]"

EMAIL 3: PROOF (Day 7)
Subject: "How [user/beta tester] got [result] with [approach]"
Body: Case study or testimonial. Specific numbers.
CTA: "Want the same result? Join our live walkthrough: [webinar link]"

EMAIL 4: WEBINAR INVITE (Day 10)
Subject: "Live session: [Specific outcome] in 60 minutes"
Body: What they'll learn. What they'll walk away with. Date/time.
CTA: "Register now — limited spots: [webinar link]"

EMAIL 5: URGENCY (Day 13, 24 hours before webinar)
Subject: "Tomorrow: [Webinar topic] — your spot"
Body: Reminder. What they'll miss if they skip. Last chance.
CTA: "Confirm your spot: [webinar link]"
```

### 3. Webinar Email Sequence — Pre & Post (DOCX)
**File:** `waitlist-webinar/webinar-email-sequence.docx`

```
PRE-WEBINAR (3 emails):

CONFIRMATION (immediately after registration):
Subject: "Confirmed: [Webinar title] — [Date]"
Body: Date, time, link, what to prepare, what they'll get.

REMINDER 1 (24 hours before):
Subject: "Tomorrow: [Topic] — here's what we'll cover"
Body: 3 bullet points of what they'll learn. Link.

REMINDER 2 (1 hour before):
Subject: "Starting in 1 hour: [Topic]"
Body: Link. "Bring your questions."

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

POST-WEBINAR (3 emails):

FOLLOW-UP 1 (same day, 2 hours after):
Subject: "Replay + everything from today's session"
Body: Replay link. Key takeaways (3 bullets). Offer recap. CTA.

FOLLOW-UP 2 (next day):
Subject: "The offer expires in [timeframe]"
Body: Recap the offer. Address top objection. Social proof. CTA.

FOLLOW-UP 3 (deadline day):
Subject: "Last chance: [Offer] closes at [time]"
Body: Final reminder. What they'll miss. CTA.
```

```
ATTENDANCE TARGET: 30-40% of registrants

```

## MENA Adaptations

- Webinar timing: 8-9 PM Gulf time works best (after work + dinner)
- WhatsApp reminders outperform email reminders 3:1 in MENA
- Arabic webinars have much less competition — consider Arabic-only or bilingual
- Ramadan scheduling: avoid iftar time, late night sessions work well during Ramadan
- Recording replay is essential — many MENA attendees watch later due to timezone spread
