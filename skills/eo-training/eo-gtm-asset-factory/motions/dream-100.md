# Dream 100 Strategy — Motion Assets

**Score context:** Fit x 0.4 + Readiness x 0.3 + MENA x 0.3
**Best for:** Founders with existing networks who want leverage through distribution partnerships, not cold grind.
**MENA Note:** Extremely strong in MENA. Networks and introductions compound here. Wasta (connections) matters.

### 2. Dream 100 Outreach Sequence (DOCX)
**File:** `dream-100/dream-100-outreach-sequence.docx`

5-touch personalized outreach sequence for Dream 100 targets. NOT cold outreach — this is warm-up + relationship building.

**Touch 1: Engage (Week 1-2)**
```
ACTION: Engage with their content for 2 weeks before reaching out
- Comment on 3-5 of their posts (substantive comments, not "great post!")
- Share one of their pieces with your own take added
- If they have a newsletter, reply to an issue with a thoughtful response

TEMPLATE (comment on their post):
"[Specific reference to what they said] — this resonates because [your experience].
We're seeing the same pattern with [your ICP]. [Add one insight they didn't mention]."
```

**Touch 2: First DM / Email (Week 3)**
```
Subject: [Specific thing they did] + a question

[First Name],

Your [specific piece of content/talk/post] on [topic] landed hard.
Especially the point about [specific detail] — I've been seeing this
play out with [your ICP context].

Quick question: [Genuine question about their expertise, not a pitch]

[Your name]
```
Rules: Under 80 words. Reference something specific. Ask a real question.

**Touch 3: Value Give (Week 4)**
```
Subject: Something for you — re: [previous topic]

[First Name],

Following up on [previous exchange].

I put together [specific value piece — could be data, an intro, a resource,
a guest post draft, a demo of something relevant to them].

Here it is: [link/attachment]

No strings. Thought you'd find it useful.

[Your name]
```
Rules: Give first, ask never. The value must be genuinely useful to THEM.

**Touch 4: The Proposal (Week 5-6)**
```
Subject: Idea — [specific collaboration concept]

[First Name],

Based on our conversations, I think there's a natural fit:

[One sentence: what you'd do together]
[One sentence: why it benefits THEIR audience]
[One sentence: what you'd handle / what you'd need from them]

Example: "[Specific collaboration format — joint webinar, co-created content,
guest episode, cross-promotion, product integration]"

Worth a 15-min call to explore? [Link]

[Your name]
```
Rules: Under 100 words. Lead with benefit to THEM. Specific proposal, not vague "let's collaborate."

**Touch 5: Follow-up (Week 7)**
```
Subject: Re: [previous subject]

[First Name],

Just circling back on the [collaboration type] idea.

If timing's off, totally understand. Happy to revisit whenever it makes sense.

Either way, keep creating — your [specific content type] is [genuine compliment].

[Your name]
```
Rules: Under 50 words. No guilt. No desperation. Leave door open.

### 3. Dream 100 Value Offer Menu (DOCX)
**File:** `dream-100/dream-100-value-offer.docx`

A menu of value exchanges the founder can offer Dream 100 targets.

**Structure:**
```
WHAT YOU CAN OFFER YOUR DREAM 100:

FOR CONTENT CREATORS:
□ Guest post / article for their platform
□ Co-host a webinar / live session
□ Provide exclusive data or case study for their content
□ Create a custom resource their audience would love
□ Appear as a guest on their podcast/show

FOR PARTNERS / COMPANIES:
□ Revenue share on referrals
□ Co-marketing campaign (split costs, share audience)
□ Product integration or API partnership
□ Joint case study showcasing mutual value
□ White-label or reseller arrangement

FOR COMMUNITY LEADERS:
□ Free access to your product for their community
□ Sponsor their event or meetup
□ Provide exclusive community-only content or workshop
□ Build a custom feature requested by their community

YOUR SPECIFIC OFFERS (customized from companyprofile.md):
1. [Based on the founder's product, what unique value can they offer?]
2. [Based on their expertise, what knowledge can they share?]
3. [Based on their network, what introductions can they facilitate?]
```

## MENA Adaptations

- WhatsApp is often more effective than email for Dream 100 in Gulf markets
- Voice notes work better than text for first approach in Saudi/UAE
- Reference mutual connections (wasta) early — it's expected, not sleazy
- Arabic-English mix is normal for business communication in Gulf
- If the target is a government-connected entity, formal Arabic may be needed for the first touch
