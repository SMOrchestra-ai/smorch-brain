## THREE-LAYER AI INSTRUCTION DESIGN

### Why Three Layers Matter

Research on AI instruction compliance shows:
- Positive framing ("always start with commercial impact") gets ~89% compliance. Negative framing ("don't start with methodology") gets ~35%.
- Behavioral instructions ("when analyzing a market, apply [framework]") outperform trait instructions ("be a senior strategist").
- Instructions at the top and bottom of a prompt get highest compliance (position effect).
- Under 300 lines total across all layers produces better results than longer instructions.
- Named reasoning frameworks ("Apply the SIGNAL-BASED GTM FRAMEWORK") scale better than detailed per-task rules.
- Verifiable rules ("always include ROI calculation") get followed more than aspirational rules ("make it high quality").

### Design Rules for All Three Instruction Files

1. **Positive over negative.** For every "avoid X" rule, provide the replacement behavior. "Use specific numbers and evidence" beats "Don't be vague."
2. **Behavioral over trait.** Write "When X condition, do Y action" not "Be X type of assistant."
3. **Critical rules at edges.** Put the 3-4 most important rules at the top and bottom of each file. Less critical rules in the middle.
4. **Verifiable in 5 seconds.** Every rule should answer: "Can I check whether Claude followed this by looking at the output for 5 seconds?"
5. **No duplication across layers.** If a rule lives in Layer 1, do not repeat it in Layer 2 or 3. Layer 3 can OVERRIDE Layer 1/2 but should not duplicate.
6. **Meta-patterns scale.** Define named frameworks in Layer 1, reference them by name in Layer 2/3. Example: Layer 1 defines "REVENUE-FIRST FILTER." Layer 3 says "Apply REVENUE-FIRST FILTER to all feature requests."

### What Goes Where (Separation Rules)

| Content Type | Layer 1 (Personal) | Layer 2 (Global) | Layer 3 (Project) |
|-------------|--------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| Founder identity, thesis | YES | Reference only | NO |
| Communication style | YES | NO | NO |
| Operating modes | YES | NO | NO |
| Tool stack | NO | YES | NO |
| Quality standards by type | NO | YES | Override only |
| Business context | NO | NO | YES |
| Technical context | NO | NO | YES |
| ICP, positioning, GTM | NO | NO | YES |
| MENA rules | YES (general) | NO | YES (project-specific) |
| Words to avoid | YES | NO | Override only |

## GAP-FILL PROTOCOL

The scorecards cover business strategy thoroughly but miss operational context (tools, workflow preferences, formatting rules, mode triggers). The gap-fill protocol collects this in 2 focused rounds of 4 questions each.

**Total: 8 questions maximum, presented in 2 rounds of 4.**

Each question feeds multiple output files to avoid redundant asking.

### Round 1: Voice, Thesis, Constraints, Modes

Present all 4 questions as a single block. Student answers all at once.

**Q1 - Thesis:** "What is the one belief about your market that most people get wrong? The contrarian take that drives everything you build."

- **Feeds:** personal-preferences.md CORE THESIS
- **Cross-ref:** positioning.md unique mechanism, brandvoice.md origin story
- **Good Answer Example:** "Most people think you need enterprise IT to scale. I believe SMEs can outcompete with signal-based selling and minimal tech debt. The contraction of decision-making to 1-3 people in 50-person companies is the unfair advantage."
- **Weak Answer Example:** "Technology is important in my market."
- **Extraction Rule:** Extract the core belief statement (2-3 sentences max). Populate personal-preferences.md CORE THESIS section. Cross-check against positioning.md unique mechanism for consistency.

**Q2 - Voice:** "When you write content (LinkedIn, YouTube, emails), which 3 words describe your tone? Give me an example of your best writing - a LinkedIn post, email intro, YouTube opener, anything."

- **Feeds:** brandvoice.md (tone guidelines, content voice examples, words to use), personal-preferences.md COMMUNICATION
- **Good Answer Example:** "Direct, contrarian, specific. Example: 'Relationship-based selling is a tax on growth. Signal-based trust engineering is the replacement. You don't need 47 coffee meetings to close enterprise deals in the Gulf — you need systematic proof of competence delivered at the exact moment intent surfaces.'"
- **Weak Answer Example:** "I write professionally. Here is my LinkedIn: [link]"
- **Extraction Rule:** Extract the 3 tone words and the writing sample. Parse sample for word choice, sentence length, pattern interrupts, and structural patterns. Populate brandvoice.md tone guidelines and content voice examples. Derive "Words to Use" from the sample vocabulary.

**Q3 - Constraints:** "What words or phrases make you cringe? And what formatting rules matter in EVERY AI response? (Examples: no em dashes, always use bullets, max response length, never start with 'Great question')"

- **Feeds:** brandvoice.md (words to avoid), personal-preferences.md HARD CONSTRAINTS
- **Good Answer Example:** "Cringe: 'leverage', 'synergy', 'holistic approach', 'at this point in time'. Formatting: max 3 sentences per paragraph, always use bullets for lists, never use em dashes, responses under 500 words except when asked for essays."
- **Weak Answer Example:** "I don't like buzzwords."
- **Extraction Rule:** Extract the full list of words/phrases to avoid. Parse formatting rules as constraint rules. Populate brandvoice.md "Words to Avoid" list. Populate personal-preferences.md HARD CONSTRAINTS with specific formatting/length rules.

**Q4 - Modes:** "When working with AI, what triggers you wanting challenge and pushback vs. wanting pure execution? Give me the switch signal - what do you say or do when you want each mode?"

- **Feeds:** personal-preferences.md OPERATING MODES
- **Good Answer Example:** "Challenge mode: 'Poke holes' or 'Play adversary.' I want strategic disagreement, not just compliance. Execution mode: 'Build this' or 'Just do it.' Pure speed, no critique. Switch signal is literal: if I say 'poke holes' you shift to challenge. If I say 'build' you go execution."
- **Weak Answer Example:** "Sometimes I want feedback, sometimes I want speed."
- **Extraction Rule:** Extract both mode triggers and their operational implications. Populate personal-preferences.md OPERATING MODES with exact trigger phrases and the expected behavior in each mode.

### Round 2: Competitors, Business Lines, Tools, Deliverables

Present all 4 questions as a single block after Round 1 answers are integrated.

**Q5 - Competitors:** "The scorecards mention these competitors: [list from SC1 Q4]. Are there MENA-specific competitors I should add? For your top 2 competitors: what is their biggest weakness?"

- **Feeds:** competitor-analysis.md (regional competitors, feature comparison)
- **Good Answer Example:** "SC1 mentioned HubSpot and Pipedrive globally. For MENA: GoHighLevel (English-heavy UI), Zoho (no Arabic), Monday.com (enterprise-only). HubSpot weakness: $120/month minimum, zero Arabic localization, assumes English-speaking ops. Pipedrive weakness: no WhatsApp integration, missing MENA payment gateways."
- **Weak Answer Example:** "They're expensive and not localized."
- **Extraction Rule:** For each new competitor, create a row in competitor-analysis.md with name and primary weakness. Cross-reference against SC1 Q4 list. Mark any duplicates as already covered. Flag regional gaps (e.g., "no MENA-specific payment method support") explicitly.

**Q6 - Business Lines:** "Do you run other business lines or products beyond [venture name]? List them with one-line descriptions."

- **Feeds:** cowork-instruction.md WHAT I BUILD, personal-preferences.md IDENTITY (if multiple ventures)
- **Good Answer Example:** "SMOrchestra.ai (main consulting + implementation arm), SalesMfast SME (Arabic-first CRM), SalesMfast Signal Engine (outbound signal stack), CXMfast AI (contact center tech), Entrepreneurs Oasis MENA (movement + training)."
- **Weak Answer Example:** "Yes, I have other projects."
- **Extraction Rule:** Extract each business line name and one-line description. Populate cowork-instruction.md WHAT I BUILD section with full list. If 3+ business lines exist, update personal-preferences.md IDENTITY to acknowledge founder-of-multiple-ventures mode. Flag which venture is primary for this project.

**Q7 - Tools:** "What tools do you use regularly? List by category: CRM, cold email, LinkedIn, automation/orchestration, scraping, design, video, AI."

- **Feeds:** cowork-instruction.md MY TOOL STACK
- **Good Answer Example:** "CRM: GoHighLevel | Cold Email: Instantly.ai | LinkedIn: HeyReach, LinkedIn Helper | Automation: n8n (self-hosted), Relevance AI | Data: Apify, Clay | Video: HeyGen | Design: Canva | AI: Claude (all interfaces)."
- **Weak Answer Example:** "I use lots of tools."
- **Extraction Rule:** Parse by category. Populate cowork-instruction.md MY TOOL STACK section with categorized list. For each tool, capture the PRIMARY category it's used in (a tool can appear once). Flag if key category is empty (e.g., no listed CRM) as a coaching note.

**Q8 - Deliverables:** "What deliverables do you produce most often? (Proposals, decks, campaigns, scripts, technical docs, landing pages, YouTube content). And do you have file naming conventions?"

- **Feeds:** cowork-instruction.md QUALITY STANDARDS + FILE NAMING
- **Good Answer Example:** "Deliverables: proposals (to clients), decks (investor and client), campaigns (cold email, LinkedIn), YouTube scripts (Arabic and English), technical docs. Naming: [client]-[type]-[date].docx for proposals, [campaign]-[channel]-v[version] for campaigns, yt-[lang]-[topic]-[date] for scripts."
- **Weak Answer Example:** "I make a lot of different things."
- **Extraction Rule:** List the top 5 deliverables by frequency. For each, extract any quality standards specific to that type (e.g., "no em dashes in proposals"). Extract file naming pattern for each type. Populate cowork-instruction.md FILE NAMING section with examples and pattern templates.

### Gap-Fill Rules

- **Presentation:** Present each round as a single block. Do not ask questions one at a time.
- **Thin Answers:** If the student gives short or incomplete answers, work with what you have. Flag thin areas in the output as "COACHING NOTE: [section] based on limited input, refine after producing real content."
- **Skip Logic:** If a question's answer is already captured in scorecard data, skip it and note why. Example: "Q6 (Business Lines) is already covered by SC1 Q2 content. Skipping."
- **Maximum Iterations:** Maximum 2 rounds. If critical data is still missing after Round 2, generate the files anyway with clear "[PLACEHOLDER]" markers.

### Consolidated Approach

The 8-question, 2-round structure is designed to:

1. **Minimize student friction:** 4 related questions at once, not 1-by-1 sequential questioning.
2. **Maximize downstream reuse:** Each answer feeds multiple output files, avoiding redundant re-asking in later skills.
3. **Cover gaps without re-running scorecards:** Scorecards are deep on strategy; gap-fill covers operational/personal context.
4. **Respect founder time:** 8 questions total takes 10-15 minutes. Focused questions, not open-ended.

### Data Quality Standards

For each gap-fill answer, apply these quality checks before extracting:

- **Named Entities:** Does the answer include specific names, tools, dates, people, companies?
- **Evidence:** Does it include examples, quotes, or specific scenarios?
- **Completeness:** If the answer feels truncated or vague, flag it in output as "[PLACEHOLDER - thin input]" and note that the field should be refined after real work is produced.
- **Specificity:** Generic answers like "I like quality work" or "it depends" should be flagged and re-asked if under 2 rounds have been used.
