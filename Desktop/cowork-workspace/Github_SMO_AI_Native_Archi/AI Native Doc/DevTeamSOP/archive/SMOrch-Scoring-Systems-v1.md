# SMOrchestra.ai Work Quality Scoring Systems
## 6 Battle-Tested Scoring Rubrics for Campaign, Offer, Copy, Social, YouTube & LinkedIn

**Version:** 1.0
**Created:** 2026-03-26
**Purpose:** Score every deliverable against expert-grade benchmarks before it ships. Kill mediocrity systematically.

---

**HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT**

Each scoring system uses a 1-10 scale per criterion, weighted by impact. The formula:

**Final Score = Sum of (Criterion Score x Weight) / Sum of All Weights**

Score interpretation:
- **9.0-10.0** — Elite. Ship it. Top 5% of what exists in market.
- **7.5-8.9** — Strong. Minor refinements, then ship.
- **6.0-7.4** — Acceptable. Needs targeted improvements on weak criteria.
- **4.0-5.9** — Below standard. Rework required before shipping.
- **Below 4.0** — Start over. Fundamental strategy or execution problem.

**Hard rule:** Nothing ships below 7.0 overall. Any single criterion below 5.0 triggers mandatory rework on that dimension regardless of overall score.

# SCORING SYSTEM 1: CAMPAIGN STRATEGY
## Hat: Battle-Tested Marketing & GTM Expert

**What this scores:** The strategic architecture of an outbound or inbound campaign before assets are produced. This is the blueprint, not the building.

**Benchmark sources:** ColdIQ methodology, Instantly.ai 2026 Benchmark Report, GTM Strategist 2025 B2B State of GTM, Signal-to-Trust framework.

### Criteria Table

| # | Criterion | Weight | What 10/10 Looks Like | What 5/10 Looks Like | What 1/10 Looks Like |
|---|-----------|--------|----------------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Signal Clarity** | 15% | Specific, detectable buying signals identified (job changes, tech stack shifts, funding rounds, RFP signals). Each signal has a detection method and freshness window (<90 days). | Generic "they might need our product" reasoning. Signals exist but no detection infrastructure. | No signal identification. "Let's email everyone in the industry." |
| 2 | **ICP Precision** | 15% | 3-Level Niche defined: Industry > Sub-segment > Trigger condition. Fit criteria include company size, tech stack, geography, budget authority. Hard Stop rules enforced (Fit=Fail means disqualify). | ICP defined at industry level only. "Mid-market SaaS companies in UAE." No trigger conditions. | No ICP. "B2B companies that could use our product." |
| 3 | **Channel-Market Fit** | 12% | Channels selected based on where ICP actually responds in MENA context (WhatsApp for warm/hot, LinkedIn for trust-building, cold email for volume at top). Channel selection backed by data or tested hypothesis. | Default Western playbook applied. "Email + LinkedIn because that's what everyone does." No MENA adaptation. | Single channel only. Or channels chosen based on what tools we have, not where buyers are. |
| 4 | **Wedge Specificity** | 12% | One-sentence wedge per week that passes the "would this make a busy VP stop scrolling?" test. Wedge derived from validated signal, not generic value prop. Different wedge per persona/segment. | Generic value proposition used as wedge. "We help companies improve their sales process." Not signal-derived. | No wedge concept. Feature-dumping or company introduction approach. |
| 5 | **Q>M>W>D Hierarchy** | 10% | Clear domino effect: Quarterly theme > Monthly campaign > Weekly wedge > Daily execution. Each level derives from the one above. Compounding impact visible. | Monthly plan exists but disconnected from quarterly strategy. Weekly execution ad hoc. | No hierarchy. Random campaigns launched when someone has an idea. |
| 6 | **Timing & Velocity** | 10% | Signal-to-first-touch under 48 hours. Sequence cadence optimized per channel (email: 3-4 day gaps, LinkedIn: staggered with email, WhatsApp: only after engagement signal). Follow-up persistence: 4-7 touches. | Reasonable cadence but no signal-speed requirement. "We'll reach out next week." | No timing strategy. Batch-and-blast monthly. No follow-up sequence. |
| 7 | **Multi-Channel Coordination** | 8% | Channels orchestrated, not parallel. LinkedIn warms before email hits. WhatsApp activates after engagement signal. Cross-channel deduplication enforced. No prospect gets hit by 3 channels same day. | Channels run independently. Marketing does email, sales does LinkedIn, no coordination. Some overlap/duplication. | Single channel or channels actively conflicting (same prospect, same day, different message). |
| 8 | **Measurement Framework** | 8% | Pre-defined KPIs per stage: reply rate targets (>5% cold email, >15% LinkedIn), meeting book rate (>2%), pipeline velocity metrics, channel-level CAC. Weekly review cadence built in. | Tracks opens and clicks. No stage-specific targets. Reviews "when we have time." | No measurement. "We'll see how it goes." |
| 9 | **Risk Mitigation** | 5% | Domain/sender reputation protection plan. Warmup strategy for new infrastructure. Fallback plan if primary channel underperforms. Compliance with Gmail/Microsoft 2026 sender requirements (DMARC, <0.1% spam complaints, <2% bounce). | Aware of deliverability but no proactive plan. "We'll watch the bounce rates." | No awareness of sender reputation, compliance, or fallback. |
| 10 | **MENA Contextualization** | 5% | Arabic/bilingual messaging where appropriate. Gulf business culture timing respected (no campaigns during Ramadan peak hours without adjustment). WhatsApp prioritized for warm touches. Social proof from regional logos/references. | English-only with minor localization. Standard Western business hours assumed. | Zero MENA adaptation. US playbook copy-pasted. |

### Campaign Strategy Score Benchmarks

**2026 Cold Email Benchmarks (Instantly.ai report):**
- Open rate: 45-65% = Good, >65% = Excellent
- Reply rate: 5-15% = Good, >10.7% = Top performer
- Meeting book rate: 1-3% = Good, >2.34% = Top performer (timeline hooks)
- Bounce rate: Must stay <2%
- Spam complaints: Must stay <0.1% for high volume senders

**2026 LinkedIn Outbound Benchmarks:**
- Connection acceptance: 25-40% = Good
- Reply to message: 15-25% = Good
- Meeting from sequence: 3-5% = Good

# SCORING SYSTEM 2: OFFER & POSITIONING
## Hat: Battle-Tested Offer & GTM Expert

**What this scores:** The core offer construct and market positioning. Is this something people would feel stupid saying no to? Is the positioning defensible and clear?

**Benchmark sources:** Alex Hormozi Value Equation ($100M Offers), April Dunford 5-Component Positioning (Obviously Awesome), Russell Brunson Attractive Character + Value Ladder, Signal-to-Trust framework.

### Criteria Table

| # | Criterion | Weight | What 10/10 Looks Like | What 5/10 Looks Like | What 1/10 Looks Like |
|---|-----------|--------|----------------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Dream Outcome Clarity** | 15% | The prospect can see themselves in a specific, measurable better future. "Go from 3 meetings/month from cold outbound to 15+ qualified meetings/month within 90 days." Outcome is vivid, tangible, desirable. | Outcome stated but vague. "Improve your sales pipeline." No specificity on magnitude or timeline. | No outcome articulated. Feature list presented as the offer. "We provide AI-powered CRM solutions." |
| 2 | **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** | 12% | Proof stacked 3+ layers deep: case studies with numbers, named logos, demo of the mechanism working, risk reversal (guarantee). Prospect thinks "this will probably work for me specifically." | One testimonial or case study. Generic "trusted by 50+ companies" without specifics. Prospect hopes it works. | Zero proof. "Trust us, we're experts." No case studies, no demo, no social proof. |
| 3 | **Time to Value** | 10% | Clear, compressed timeline. "First signals detected in week 1. First meetings booked by week 3. Full pipeline running by day 60." Milestones defined. | Vague timeline. "Results typically appear within a few months." No milestones. | No timeline. Or worse: "This is a long-term strategic investment." Translation: you won't see ROI. |
| 4 | **Effort & Sacrifice Minimization** | 10% | Offer designed so the buyer does minimal work. "We build it, we run it, we hand you booked meetings. Your team spends 30 minutes/week reviewing pipeline." Done-for-you or done-with-you with clear scope. | Buyer has to do significant work but it's somewhat guided. "We'll train your team and provide templates." | Buyer does all the work. Essentially selling a course or toolkit disguised as a service. No support structure. |
| 5 | **Unique Mechanism** | 12% | Proprietary or named framework that explains WHY this works differently. "Signal-to-Trust Engine" or "Digital Silence Index." Mechanism is believable, explainable in 60 seconds, and defensible. | Generic methodology. "We use AI and data-driven insights." No named framework. Nothing proprietary. | No mechanism. "We do outbound." Zero differentiation in how. |
| 6 | **Competitive Alternative Clarity** | 8% | Crystal clear on what happens if they don't buy: they keep doing manual relationship-selling (47 coffee meetings), they hire 2 more SDRs ($180K+ loaded cost), or they use a generic tool and get generic results. Alternative is painful. | Mentions competitors exist but doesn't frame the alternative vividly. "Other agencies do this differently." | No competitive framing. Prospect has no context for why this vs anything else. |
| 7 | **Price-to-Value Gap** | 10% | Price feels like a fraction of the value. If you deliver 15 meetings/month and their average deal is $50K, paying $5K/month is 10x ROI minimum. The math is explicit in the offer. | Value is implied but not calculated. "This will pay for itself." No specific ROI math. | Price feels arbitrary. No value anchor. "Our package starts at $X." Why X? |
| 8 | **Risk Reversal** | 8% | Strong guarantee: performance-based, money-back on specific conditions, or pilot/proof-of-concept with clear success criteria. Buyer risk approaches zero. | Soft guarantee. "We'll work with you until you're satisfied." No specific conditions. | No guarantee. All risk on buyer. "Payment due upfront, no refunds." |
| 9 | **ICP-Offer Alignment** | 8% | Offer is built for a specific buyer with specific pain. Enterprise B2B in Gulf with 50+ person sales team trying to enter new market = different offer than SME in Dubai wanting more WhatsApp leads. Segmented offers exist. | One offer for everyone. "Our solution works for businesses of all sizes." No segmentation. | Offer is misaligned with actual buyer needs. Built for who you want to sell to, not who actually buys. |
| 10 | **Positioning Statement Clarity** | 7% | Passes the "bar test": if someone asks at a networking event "what do you do?", the answer takes 15 seconds and the person immediately knows if they're a prospect or not. No jargon. Crystal clear category. | Takes 60+ seconds to explain. Uses industry jargon. Person nods politely but doesn't really get it. | Cannot articulate what you do without a slide deck. Positioning is a paragraph, not a sentence. |

### Offer Quality Benchmarks

**Hormozi Value Equation Target:**
- Dream Outcome (maximize) x Perceived Likelihood (maximize) / Time Delay (minimize) x Effort & Sacrifice (minimize) = perceived value
- Score each numerator 1-10, each denominator 1-10 (inverted: 10 = minimal delay/effort)
- Target: numerator product > 60, denominator product < 15 = ratio > 4.0

**Dunford Positioning Completeness Check:**
All 5 components must be explicitly defined: (1) Competitive alternatives, (2) Unique attributes, (3) Value/proof, (4) Target customer characteristics, (5) Market category. Missing any = incomplete positioning.

# SCORING SYSTEM 3: COPYWRITING
## Hat: Battle-Tested Copywriter

**What this scores:** The actual written words in cold emails, VSL scripts, and LinkedIn outreach messages. Evaluates whether copy will survive spam filters, stop the scroll, engage the reader, and trigger action.

**Benchmark sources:** Instantly.ai 2026 Cold Email Benchmarks, CopyPosse VSL framework, LinkedIn 2026 engagement data, Gmail/Microsoft 2026 spam filter mechanics.

### 3A: COLD EMAIL COPY SCORING

| # | Criterion | Weight | What 10/10 Looks Like | What 5/10 Looks Like | What 1/10 Looks Like |
|---|-----------|--------|----------------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Subject Line** | 15% | 3-6 words. Lowercase. Feels like an internal email, not marketing. Creates curiosity gap without clickbait. Personalized variable (company name or trigger). Zero spam trigger words. Example: "quick q about {{company}} expansion" | Generic but not terrible. "Partnership opportunity" or "Quick question." No personalization. | Marketing blast subject. "Unlock Your Growth Potential with AI-Powered Solutions!" All caps, exclamation, spam triggers. |
| 2 | **Opening Line (Hook)** | 15% | Signal-derived. References something specific the prospect DID (hired a VP Sales, raised Series B, posted about X challenge, expanded to new market). Timeline hook: "Noticed {{company}} just {{signal}}..." 2.3x higher reply rate than problem hooks. | Generic problem statement. "Many companies in your industry struggle with..." Not personalized. Not signal-based. | "I hope this email finds you well." Or "My name is X and I work at Y." Instant delete. |
| 3 | **Spam Filter Survival** | 12% | Zero spam trigger words (free, guarantee, act now, limited time, click here). No HTML formatting. No images or links in first email. Plain text look. Proper DMARC/DKIM/SPF on sending domain. Under 80 words. No ALL CAPS. | Mostly clean but 1-2 minor triggers. Maybe one link. Slightly formatted. 100-150 words. | Loaded with spam triggers. HTML template. Multiple links. Images. Looks like a newsletter. Over 200 words. Gmail sends to spam or rejects. |
| 4 | **Body Value Density** | 12% | Every sentence adds value or moves toward CTA. No filler. Demonstrates understanding of their specific situation. Shows proof of competence in 1-2 sentences max. Total email: 50-80 words. | Some value but padded. "We've helped companies like yours..." without specifics. 100-150 words with unnecessary context. | Feature dump. Company history. "Founded in 2015, we are a leading provider of..." No value for the reader. |
| 5 | **Social Proof Integration** | 8% | Subtle, specific, relevant. "Did this for {{similar company}} — went from X to Y in Z weeks." One line. Named or industry-specific. | Generic. "Trusted by 100+ companies." No specifics. Or proof not relevant to prospect's situation. | No social proof. Or bragging: "We're the #1 rated platform on G2." |
| 6 | **CTA Clarity & Friction** | 15% | Single, low-friction CTA. "Worth a 15-min call this week?" or "Want me to send a 2-min video showing how this would work for {{company}}?" Binary yes/no. No calendar link in first email. | Reasonable CTA but slightly high friction. "Let's schedule a 30-minute demo." Or multiple CTAs. | No CTA. Or high friction: "Fill out this form and our team will get back to you." Or aggressive: "Ready to 10x your revenue?" |
| 7 | **Personalization Depth** | 10% | 3 layers: (1) Signal/trigger, (2) Company-specific detail, (3) Role-specific pain. Not just {{firstName}} merge. Feels like a human wrote this for one person. | First name + company name merge. Maybe industry mention. Feels semi-personalized. | Zero personalization. Mass blast obvious. Or fake personalization: "I've been following {{company}} for a while" when clearly not. |
| 8 | **Sequence Architecture** | 8% | 4-7 emails. Each follow-up adds NEW value (different angle, new proof point, alternative CTA). 3-4 day spacing. Tone shifts from curious to helpful to direct across sequence. Step 1 gets 58% of replies; steps 2-7 add 42%. | 3 emails. Follow-ups are "just checking in" or "bumping this to the top." No new value per touch. | 1 email, no follow-up. Or 10+ emails that repeat the same pitch. Or follow-ups within 24 hours (harassment pattern). |
| 9 | **Tone & Voice** | 5% | Peer-to-peer. Not supplicant ("I'd be honored if you'd spare 5 minutes"). Not aggressive ("You're losing $X every day you wait"). Confident, casual, direct. Reads like a sharp colleague, not a salesperson. | Professional but stiff. Corporate tone. "I would like to introduce..." Reads like a business letter from 2010. | Desperate, aggressive, or sycophantic. "I know you're incredibly busy but..." or "ACT NOW before your competitors do!" |

### Cold Email Score Benchmarks (2026)
- **Reply rate by score tier:** 9-10 score = 10-15% reply rate expected. 7-8 = 5-10%. 5-6 = 2-5%. Below 5 = <2%.
- **Timeline hooks outperform problem hooks 2.3x** on reply rate and 3.4x on meeting booking rate (Instantly 2026 data).
- **Optimal email length:** 50-80 words. Every word beyond 80 decreases reply probability.
- **Follow-up impact:** 58% of replies come from email 1; 42% from follow-ups. Stopping at 1-2 emails leaves nearly half your results on the table.

### VSL Score Benchmarks
- **Conversion rate:** Top VSLs convert at 5-15% of viewers to next step. Average is 2-5%. Below 2% = script problem.
- **Watch time:** 50%+ average view duration = strong. 30-50% = needs work. <30% = hook or retention failure.
- **VSL funnels outperform text-only sales pages by 20-50%** on conversion when script is strong.

# SCORING SYSTEM USAGE PROTOCOL

1. **Before creating any deliverable:** Identify which scoring system applies. Review the criteria before starting work, not after.

2. **Self-score during creation:** Check alignment with 10/10 criteria as you build. Don't finish and then evaluate: evaluate as you go.

3. **Final score before shipping:** Run the full scorecard. Be honest. The scoring system only works if scores are truthful, not flattering.

4. **Calibration:** Every 4 weeks, pick 3 shipped deliverables and re-score them with hindsight (actual performance data vs predicted score). Adjust criteria weights if certain criteria are over/under-predicting quality.

5. **Score trending:** Track scores over time per scoring system. Are we improving? Where are we consistently weak?

6. **Hard stop enforcement:** Any single criterion below 5.0 = mandatory rework on that dimension. No exceptions. A campaign with a 9.0 average but a 4.0 on "Spam Filter Survival" will fail in practice regardless of other strengths.
