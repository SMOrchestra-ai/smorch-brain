# YouTube Scorer

**System 5 of 6 — Battle-Tested YouTube Personal Branding Expert Hat**

**What this scores:** YouTube thumbnail, title, script, and description quality for an educational/authority-building channel. YouTube is a search + recommendation engine; content must be optimized for both discovery (title, thumbnail, description) and retention (script, pacing).

**Benchmark sources:** VidIQ, TubeBuddy, YouTube Studio 2025 A/B testing data, Retention Rabbit 2025 Benchmark Report, YouTube Algorithm 2026 mechanics. Read `references/benchmarks-2026.md` for current numbers.

**Scoring rules:** Read `references/score-bands.md` for universal score bands, hard stop rules, and output formats.

## SUBSYSTEM 5A: THUMBNAIL — 7 Criteria

The thumbnail is the billboard. At 120x90 pixels on mobile, it has less than 1 second to communicate a reason to click.

### C1: Visual Clarity at Small Size — Weight: 20%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Immediately readable at 120x90px (mobile thumbnail size). Clear focal point. Max 2-3 elements. Brain processes the message in <1 second. |
| 7/10 | Good | Readable on mobile. 3-4 elements. Focal point clear but takes a moment to parse. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Readable but busy. 4-5 elements competing for attention. Needs a moment to parse. |
| 1/10 | Failure | Cluttered, unreadable at small size. Text too small. Too many elements. |

**Fix Action:** Remove everything except the face/focal element + 0-3 text words. Test: shrink to 120x90px. If you can't read it instantly, simplify.

---

### C2: Emotional Expression — Weight: 20%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Face showing strong, genuine emotion (surprise, curiosity, intensity). Emotional expressions increase CTR by up to 30%. Eyes visible and engaging. If no face: strong visual metaphor with emotional charge. |
| 7/10 | Good | Face present with clear emotion. May not be as striking but communicates energy. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Face present but neutral expression. Or stock photo quality. No emotional pull. |
| 1/10 | Failure | No face, no emotion, no visual hook. Corporate slide thumbnail. Flat design. |

**Fix Action:** Retake the photo with exaggerated expression (surprise, intensity, or confusion). Emotion must be visible at small size. If using graphics instead of face, add high-contrast visual tension (before/after, arrows, X marks).

---

### C3: Text Optimization — Weight: 15%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | 0-3 words maximum. Bold, high-contrast, sans-serif font. Text complements (not duplicates) the title. Creates curiosity gap with title. |
| 7/10 | Good | 3-4 words. Clear and readable. Adds to the thumbnail without crowding. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | 4-6 words. Acceptable for tutorial content. Slightly too much to process quickly. |
| 1/10 | Failure | 7+ words. Sentence on a thumbnail. Unreadable on mobile. Or duplicates the title exactly. |

**Fix Action:** Cut text to 3 words max. If the text duplicates the title, remove it entirely. The thumbnail text should create a gap the title completes.

---

### C4: Color Contrast & Palette — Weight: 15%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | High contrast between elements. Stands out in YouTube feed. Bold colors (yellows, oranges, blues). Background doesn't blend with YouTube's white/dark interface. |
| 7/10 | Good | Good contrast. Stands out in most feeds. May blend slightly in dark mode or light mode. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Adequate contrast but doesn't pop. Blends with adjacent thumbnails. Muted palette. |
| 1/10 | Failure | Low contrast. Dark image with dark text. Or so garish it looks unprofessional. |

**Fix Action:** Add a colored border or background that contrasts with YouTube's interface. Yellow and orange pop in both light and dark mode. Test in both modes.

---

### C5: Brand Consistency — Weight: 10%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Recognizable template: consistent style, color scheme, composition. Viewers know it's your video before reading the title. |
| 7/10 | Good | Mostly consistent. Recognizable pattern with minor variations. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Inconsistent. Each thumbnail looks like a different channel. No brand building. |
| 1/10 | Failure | No brand recognition. Stock images or auto-generated thumbnails. |

**Fix Action:** Create a thumbnail template: consistent background color, consistent font, consistent face placement. Use it for 10+ videos before changing.

---

### C6: Curiosity Gap — Weight: 10%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Thumbnail creates a question in the viewer's mind that the title completes (or vice versa). Together they create irresistible "I need to know" feeling. |
| 7/10 | Good | Thumbnail and title work together. Some curiosity generated. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Thumbnail and title are redundant. Both say the same thing. No gap. |
| 1/10 | Failure | Disconnected. Or thumbnail reveals the answer, killing the reason to click. |

**Fix Action:** Show the REACTION or RESULT in the thumbnail (shocked face, big number, before/after). Let the title explain the CONTEXT. Gap = "Why that reaction?" + Title answers.

---

### C7: A/B Test Readiness — Weight: 10%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | 2-3 variants designed for YouTube Studio A/B testing. Different approaches (with face vs without, different text, different color) ready to test. |
| 7/10 | Good | 2 variants available. Different enough to test meaningfully. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | One version only. No testing plan. |
| 1/10 | Failure | Untestable. One generic thumbnail with no strategic thinking. |

**Fix Action:** Create 2 variants: one with face (emotion) and one without (graphic/text focus). Run YouTube Studio's built-in A/B test. 20% CTR lift is the average for creators who test.

## SUBSYSTEM 5B: TITLE — 6 Criteria

### C1: Curiosity Trigger — Weight: 25%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Irresistible "I need to know." Curiosity gap, surprising claim, or counterintuitive framing. "Why Your Best Clients Will Never Reply to Cold Email" |
| 7/10 | Good | Clear interest driver. Specific enough to promise value. "How We Book 15 Meetings a Month in Gulf Markets" |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Informational but not curiosity-driven. "How to Improve Your Cold Email Strategy." |
| 1/10 | Failure | Boring. "Cold Email Tips." Or so vague: "Some Thoughts on Business." |

**Fix Action:** Add one of: a specific number, a counterintuitive claim, or a "how/why" that promises revelation. Turn "Cold Email Strategy" into "Why 80% of Cold Emails Die Before the Inbox (And the 3-Line Fix)."

---

### C2: Specificity — Weight: 20%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Specific numbers, timeframes, contexts. "$0 to $50K MRR in 90 Days: The Exact Gulf Market Playbook." |
| 7/10 | Good | Some specificity. "How I Grew Outbound Pipeline in 3 Months." Clear but could be sharper on the numbers. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Somewhat specific but could be sharper. "How I Grew My Business Fast." |
| 1/10 | Failure | Completely vague. "Business Growth Strategy." |

**Fix Action:** Add one number (dollar amount, percentage, timeframe, or count). Numbers build trust and CTR simultaneously.

---

### C3: Keyword Integration — Weight: 15%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Target keyword appears naturally in first 60 characters. Searchers and algorithm both satisfied. Keyword matches actual search volume. |
| 7/10 | Good | Keyword present and natural. May not be in first 60 chars but within the title. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Keyword present but awkward placement. Stuffed or unnatural. |
| 1/10 | Failure | No keyword. Or keyword so obscure nobody searches for it. |

**Fix Action:** Identify the primary search term your audience uses. Place it in the first 60 characters of the title. Check search volume with VidIQ/TubeBuddy.

---

### C4: Length Optimization — Weight: 10%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | 50-70 characters. Fully visible on mobile and desktop. No truncation. Every word earns its place. |
| 7/10 | Good | 60-80 characters. Minor truncation on mobile possible but key info preserved. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | 70-90 characters. Gets truncated on mobile. Key information cut off. |
| 1/10 | Failure | Over 100 characters. Title is a paragraph. Mobile shows half. |

**Fix Action:** If over 70 characters, identify which words add the least value and cut them. Test: does the truncated version (first 60 chars) still communicate the promise?

---

### C5: Promise Clarity — Weight: 15%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Viewer knows exactly what they'll get from watching. Clear value promise. Believable and desirable. |
| 7/10 | Good | Promise clear but could be more compelling. Viewer understands the topic but may not feel urgency to click. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Promise vague. "Learn About Signal-Based GTM." What will I know after watching? |
| 1/10 | Failure | No promise. "Episode 7: Discussion with Ahmed." Zero reason to click. |

**Fix Action:** Complete this: "After watching, the viewer will be able to [specific action/knowledge]." If you can't finish the sentence specifically, the promise isn't clear.

---

### C6: Thumbnail-Title Synergy — Weight: 15%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | 1+1=3 effect. Thumbnail shows emotional reaction; title reveals context. Together, irresistible. |
| 7/10 | Good | Thumbnail and title complement each other. Work together but not magical. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Redundant. Both communicate the same thing. |
| 1/10 | Failure | Disconnected. Viewer confused about what the video is about. |

**Fix Action:** Ensure thumbnail and title answer different questions: thumbnail = "What happened?" (emotion/result), title = "Why/How?" (context/promise).

## SUBSYSTEM 5C: SCRIPT — 8 Criteria

### C1: Hook (First 15 Seconds) — Weight: 20%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Bold claim, specific promise, or pattern interrupt that earns the next 30 seconds. "In the next 10 minutes, I'll show you the exact system that books 15+ meetings a month in the Gulf — without a single coffee meeting." 80-90% retention at 30s. |
| 7/10 | Good | Clear value promise within 10 seconds. Gets to the point. Specific enough to hold attention. 70-80% retention at 30s. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Decent opening but slow. "Today we're going to talk about..." Takes 30+ seconds for value. 60-70% retention. |
| 1/10 | Failure | "Hey everyone, welcome back, if you're new here make sure to subscribe..." 40-50% retention. |

**Fix Action:** Rewrite first 2 sentences: sentence 1 = bold claim or specific pain. Sentence 2 = "In the next [X] minutes, I'll show you [specific deliverable]." Delete any intro fluff.

---

### C2: Retention Architecture — Weight: 15%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Pattern interrupts every 60-90 seconds. Open loops: "In a minute I'll show you..." Mid-point retention hook at 50%. Pacing varies. |
| 7/10 | Good | Pattern interrupts every 2 minutes. Some open loops. Pacing varies but could be sharper. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Linear delivery. Some visual changes but predictable. No open loops. Retention drops steadily after minute 2. |
| 1/10 | Failure | Talking head, same framing, same energy for 10+ minutes. |

**Fix Action:** Mark 5 points in the script for: (1) open loop before each major section, (2) visual change or B-roll cut, (3) energy shift (question to audience, story, or data reveal).

---

### C3: Value Delivery — Weight: 15%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Actionable frameworks, specific examples, real data. Viewer can implement something after watching. Teaches through showing, not telling. |
| 7/10 | Good | Good information with some actionable elements. Mix of theory and practice. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Theoretical. "You should focus on value-based selling." No specifics. |
| 1/10 | Failure | No value. All self-promotion. Or so basic it's Google-able in 10 seconds. |

**Fix Action:** For each main point, add: (1) A specific example from your experience, (2) A number or benchmark, (3) A "here's exactly how" step the viewer can copy.

---

### C4: Story Integration — Weight: 10%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Personal or client stories woven into teaching. Situation > Problem > Turning point > Result structure. Emotional resonance. Makes abstract concepts concrete. |
| 7/10 | Good | Stories present. May not follow full structure but add context. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Facts without story. True but not memorable. |
| 1/10 | Failure | No stories. Pure lecture. Or fabricated stories. |

**Fix Action:** Add one real story per major point: "Last month, a client in [city] told me [specific thing]. Here's what happened when we [applied the framework]..."

---

### C5: Script Length & Pacing — Weight: 10%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | 5-10 minutes for educational content (highest retention at 31.5% average). 1,200-2,000 words. No section drags. |
| 7/10 | Good | Within 5-15 minute range. Mostly well-paced. One section may run slightly long. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Too long (15+) or too short (<3). Sections that drag. |
| 1/10 | Failure | 2-minute overview or 25-minute lecture. |

**Fix Action:** Target 7-8 minutes. If over 10, identify which section can be cut or split into a separate video. If under 5, add one more example or story per section.

---

### C6: CTA Integration — Weight: 10%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | CTAs feel natural. "If you want the template I just showed, link in description." Appears 2-3 times: mid-video (value peak), end, verbal mention. |
| 7/10 | Good | CTA at 2 points. Natural delivery. One mid-video, one at end. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | One CTA at end. "Don't forget to subscribe." Most viewers already left. |
| 1/10 | Failure | No CTA. Or aggressive CTA every 60 seconds. |

**Fix Action:** Place CTA after your strongest proof point (mid-video, when engagement peaks). Repeat at end. Make it contextual: "If you want [thing I just showed], [link/action]."

---

### C7: Contrarian Angle — Weight: 10%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Challenges conventional wisdom with evidence. "Everyone says build relationships first. I'll show you why that's the most expensive advice in B2B." Creates debate. |
| 7/10 | Good | Fresh perspective on a known topic. Not just repeating common advice. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Conventional take. True but not differentiated. |
| 1/10 | Failure | Contrarian without evidence. Or so safe it's boring. |

**Fix Action:** Identify the most common advice about your topic. Take the opposite position. Back it with one specific data point or experience. "Everyone says [X]. Here's why [opposite] works better."

---

### C8: Language & Audience Match — Weight: 10%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Perfect match for target viewer. Arabic content: conversational Gulf tone with English tech terms. English content: direct, slightly provocative, expert but accessible. |
| 7/10 | Good | Language correct and appropriate. Tone slightly off for YouTube (too formal, too casual). |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Correct but tone mismatch. Too formal for YouTube. Reads like a whitepaper. |
| 1/10 | Failure | Wrong language for audience. Or corporate presentation style on personal brand channel. |

**Fix Action:** Read the script out loud. If it sounds like a presentation, rewrite it as a conversation. YouTube is conversational, not corporate.

## SUBSYSTEM 5D: DESCRIPTION — 5 Criteria

### C1: First 2 Lines (Above Fold) — Weight: 30%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Compelling summary with keyword in 150 characters. Appears in search results. Hook + keyword + value promise. |
| 7/10 | Good | Summary with keyword. Clear and relevant. May not be as compelling as possible. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Generic. "In this video I discuss outbound strategies." |
| 1/10 | Failure | Empty or copy-pasted from another video. |

**Fix Action:** Rewrite first line as: "[Keyword phrase]: [specific value promise in this video]." Under 150 characters. This is what shows in search.

---

### C2: Timestamps/Chapters — Weight: 20%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Detailed chapter markers for every major section. Helps retention. Viewers skip to what they need. |
| 7/10 | Good | Key timestamps present. 4-6 chapters covering main sections. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Minimal. 2-3 markers. Doesn't cover all sections. |
| 1/10 | Failure | No timestamps. No navigation. |

**Fix Action:** Add timestamps for each major section: "0:00 [Hook] / 1:30 [Problem] / 3:00 [Framework] / 5:00 [Demo] / 7:00 [CTA]."

---

### C3: SEO Keywords — Weight: 15%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Primary + secondary keywords woven naturally. Long-tail variations. 200-500 words. Keywords 3-5 times naturally. |
| 7/10 | Good | Keywords present and natural. 150-300 words. 2-3 keyword placements. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Some keywords but not strategic. Too short (<100 words) or stuffed. |
| 1/10 | Failure | No keywords. Description is 1 sentence or just links. |

**Fix Action:** Write a 200-word description that naturally includes the primary keyword 3 times and 2 secondary keywords. Use complete sentences, not keyword lists.

---

### C4: Links & Resources — Weight: 15%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Relevant links in logical order: primary CTA first, mentioned resources, social profiles, related videos. |
| 7/10 | Good | Key links present. Primary CTA linked. Social profiles included. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Some links but disorganized. Homepage link instead of specific landing page. |
| 1/10 | Failure | No links. Or broken links. |

**Fix Action:** Order links by priority: (1) CTA/resource link, (2) related video, (3) social profiles. Use specific landing pages, never a homepage.

---

### C5: Content Funnel Connection — Weight: 20%

| Level | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10 | Excellence | Drives viewers deeper: related video, playlist, free resource, community. Creates content journey. |
| 7/10 | Good | Links to 1-2 related resources. Some funnel thinking. |
| 5/10 | Mediocre | Mentions other content vaguely. No specific links. |
| 1/10 | Failure | Video is a standalone dead end. No path to next engagement. |

**Fix Action:** Add: (1) "Watch next: [specific related video with link]", (2) "Download: [free resource related to video topic]", (3) "Join: [community/newsletter/training]."

## Scoring Execution

### Input Required
1. The YouTube content to score (thumbnail image, title text, script, description)
2. Which subsystem(s) — auto-detect from what's provided
3. Target language/audience (Arabic or English, MENA or global)

### Scoring Mindset

Think like a YouTube growth strategist who has analyzed 10,000+ videos and their analytics. You know that:
- The thumbnail is responsible for 60%+ of a video's click-through rate
- 5-10 minute videos hold viewers best at 31.5% average retention
- The hook must deliver the value promise in the first 15 seconds
- YouTube Studio A/B testing on thumbnails yields 20% average CTR lift
- Custom thumbnails outperform auto-generated by 60-70%
- Emotional expressions increase CTR by 30%
- Open loops increase mid-video retention by 15-25%

## Cross-System Dependencies

YouTube content draws from offer positioning and campaign strategy. Weak upstream systems produce weak videos.

| Low Score In YouTube | Likely Upstream Cause | Check This System & Criterion |
|---------------------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| 5C C1: Hook has no compelling claim | No clear dream outcome | Offer/Positioning: C1 Dream Outcome Clarity |
| 5C C3: Value delivery thin | No framework to teach | Offer/Positioning: C5 Unique Mechanism |
| 5C C4: No stories to tell | No case studies built | Offer/Positioning: C2 Perceived Likelihood |
| 5C C7: No contrarian angle | Positioning too conventional | Offer/Positioning: C10 Positioning Statement |
| 5B C1: Title not curiosity-driven | Wedge not specific enough | Campaign Strategy: C4 Wedge Specificity |
| 5D C5: No funnel connection | No content hierarchy | Campaign Strategy: C5 Q>M>W>D Hierarchy |
| 5C C8: Language mismatch | ICP not defined clearly | Campaign Strategy: C2 ICP Precision |
| All subsystems low | YouTube not in channel mix | Campaign Strategy: C3 Channel-Market Fit |

YouTube retention problems often trace to offer positioning: if you can't articulate a unique mechanism clearly, no amount of editing will fix viewer drop-off.
