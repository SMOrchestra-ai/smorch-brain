## Cold Email Benchmarks (Instantly.ai 2026)

| Metric | Below Average | Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|--------------|---------|------|-----------|
| Open rate | <30% | 30-45% | 45-65% | >65% |
| Reply rate | <2% | 2-5% | 5-10% | >10.7% |
| Meeting book rate | <0.5% | 0.5-1% | 1-3% | >2.34% |
| Bounce rate | >5% | 2-5% | <2% | <0.5% |
| Spam complaints | >0.3% | 0.1-0.3% | <0.1% | <0.05% |

**Key findings:**
- Timeline hooks outperform problem hooks 2.3x on reply rate and 3.4x on meeting booking
- Optimal email length: 50-80 words (every word beyond 80 decreases reply probability)
- Follow-up impact: 58% of replies from email 1, 42% from follow-ups (steps 2-7)
- Personalized first lines increase reply rate by 32%
- Step 1 gets majority of replies, but stopping at 1-2 emails leaves ~42% on the table

**Spam filter mechanics (Gmail/Microsoft 2026):**
- DMARC enforcement is now mandatory for bulk senders
- Spam complaint rate must stay <0.1% for high-volume senders
- One-click unsubscribe required for marketing emails
- Domain age <30 days = heavy throttling
- HTML-heavy emails see 15-20% lower inbox placement vs plain text

## Composite Campaign Health Formula

When multiple scoring systems apply to a single campaign, calculate Campaign Health:

```
Campaign Health = (Campaign Strategy x 0.25) +
                  (Offer/Positioning x 0.20) +
                  (Best Copywriting Subsystem x 0.25) +
                  (Social Media x 0.15) +
                  (YouTube OR LinkedIn x 0.15)
```

Use the highest-scoring applicable system for the last slot.

| Campaign Health | Level | Meaning |
|----------------|-------|---------|
| 8.5+ | GREEN | Full deployment, scale budget |
| 7.0-8.4 | YELLOW | Deploy with monitoring, fix weak links |
| 5.5-6.9 | ORANGE | Fix before deployment |
| Below 5.5 | RED | Campaign not ready, major rework |
